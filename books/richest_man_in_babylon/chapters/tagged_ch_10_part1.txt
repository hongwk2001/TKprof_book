The Walls of Babylon

Old Banzar was a grim warrior from another era. He stood guard at the passageway leading to the top of the ancient walls of Babylon. Up above, brave defenders were battling to hold the walls. The future existence of this great city depended entirely on them. Hundreds of thousands of citizens were relying on their protection.

The roar of the attacking armies echoed over the walls. Men yelled, thousands of horses trampled the ground, and battering rams pounded against the bronze gates with a deafening boom.

Spearmen lounged in the street behind the gate. They were waiting to defend the entrance if the gates gave way. However, there were only a few of them for such a massive task. The main armies of Babylon were far away in the east. They were with their king on a great expedition against the Elamites. No one expected an attack on the city while they were gone. Because of this, the defending forces were small. Then, the mighty Assyrian armies unexpectedly attacked from the north. Now, the walls had to hold, or Babylon was doomed.

Great crowds of pale, terrified citizens gathered around Banzar. They were eagerly looking for news of the battle. They watched in quiet awe as a steady stream of dead and wounded soldiers was carried out of the passageway.

This gate was the crucial point of the attack. The enemy had circled the city for three days. Then, they suddenly threw all their great strength against this specific section.

The defenders at the top of the wall fought back fiercely. They used arrows and burning oil to stop the enemy's climbing platforms and scaling ladders. If any attackers reached the top, the defenders fought them off with spears. Meanwhile, thousands of enemy archers fired a deadly barrage of arrows back at the defenders.

Old Banzar had the best spot to get news. He was closest to the fighting. He was always the first to hear when the frenzied attackers were pushed back.

<rest>Go back to your husband,</rest> Banzar told her. <rest>Tell him the gates are strong and will withstand the rams. Tell him that the climbers who scale the walls are only met with our waiting spears. Now watch your step, and hurry back behind the buildings.</rest>

Banzar stepped aside to clear the passageway for some heavily armed reinforcements. They marched by with a heavy tread, their bronze shields clanking. As they passed, a small girl pulled at his belt.

The grim old soldier blinked his eyes and pushed his chin forward as he looked down at the child.

<rest>Do not be afraid, little one,</rest> he reassured her. <rest>The walls of Babylon will protect you. They will protect your mother, your little brother, and the baby. The good Queen Semiramis built these walls over a hundred years ago to keep people like you safe. The walls have never been broken through. Go back home. Tell your mother, your little brother, and the baby that the walls of Babylon will protect them. Tell them they have nothing to fear.</rest>

Day after day, old Banzar stood at his post. He watched the reinforcements march up the passageway. They would stay up there and fight until they came back down wounded or dead. Throngs of frightened citizens crowded around him constantly. They were all eager to know if the walls would hold. Banzar answered everyone with the quiet dignity of an old soldier: <rest>The walls of Babylon will protect you.</rest>

The attack raged on for three weeks and five days with barely a pause. Banzar's jaw set harder and grimmer. The passageway behind him was wet with the blood of the wounded. The ground turned into thick mud from the endless streams of men marching up and staggering back down. Every day, the slaughtered attackers piled up in heaps in front of the wall. Every night, their comrades carried the bodies back and buried them. Finally, on the fifth night of the fourth week, the noise outside faded away. The first streaks of daylight lit up the plains. They revealed massive clouds of dust kicked up by the retreating enemy armies.

A mighty shout went up from the defenders. There was no mistaking what it meant. The waiting troops behind the walls repeated the cheer. The citizens in the streets echoed it back. The sound swept over the entire city with the force of a violent storm.

People rushed out of their houses. The streets quickly jammed with a thrilled, pulsing mob. Their pent-up fear from the past month finally exploded into a wild chorus of joy. Flames of victory burst forth from the high tower of the Temple of Bel. A column of blue smoke floated high into the sky to carry the good news far and wide.

The walls of Babylon had once again defeated a massive, vicious enemy. The foe had been determined to steal the city's rich treasures. They had wanted to ravish and enslave her citizens.

Babylon endured century after century because it was fully protected. The city simply could not afford to be anything else.

The walls of Babylon were an outstanding example of humanity's need and desire for protection. This desire is a natural part of the human race. It is just as strong today as it ever was in ancient times. However, today we have developed broader and better plans to achieve the exact same goal.

In modern times, we have our own impregnable walls. We use insurance, savings accounts, and dependable investments to protect ourselves. These modern walls guard us against the unexpected tragedies that can suddenly enter our lives and sit down at our firesides.
