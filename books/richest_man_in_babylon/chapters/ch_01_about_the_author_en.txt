About the Author

George Samuel Clason was born in Louisiana, Missouri, on November 7, 1874. He attended the University of Nebraska and served in the United States Army during the Spanish-American War.

He started a long career in publishing. He founded the Clason Map Company in Denver, Colorado, and published the first road atlas covering the United States and Canada.

In 1926, he released the first of a famous series of pamphlets. These pamphlets focused on saving money and achieving financial success. He used stories, called parables, set in ancient Babylon to illustrate his ideas.

Banks and insurance companies distributed these pamphlets in large numbers. Millions of people became familiar with them. The most famous one was "The Richest Man in Babylon." This particular story is where the current book gets its title. These "Babylonian parables" have become a modern inspirational classic.
