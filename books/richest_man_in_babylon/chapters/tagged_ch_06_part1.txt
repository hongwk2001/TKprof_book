Seven Cures For a Lean Purse

The glory of Babylon endures. Through the ages, its reputation comes to us as the richest of cities, with fabulous treasures.

But it wasn't always like this. The wealth of Babylon was the result of its people's wisdom. First, they had to learn how to become wealthy.

When the Good King Sargon returned to Babylon after defeating his enemies, the Elamites, he faced a serious situation. The Royal Chancellor explained it to the King like this:

<rest>Your Majesty, we brought our people many years of great prosperity by building the great irrigation canals and the mighty temples of the Gods. But now that those works are finished, the people seem unable to support themselves.</rest>

<rest>The laborers have no jobs. The merchants have few customers. The farmers can't sell their produce. The people don't have enough gold to buy food.</rest>

The King demanded, <rest>But where has all the gold gone that we spent on these great improvements?</rest>

The Chancellor responded, <rest>I fear it has found its way into the hands of a few very rich men in our city. It filtered through the fingers of most of our people as quickly as goat's milk goes through a strainer. Now that the stream of gold has stopped flowing, most of our people have nothing to show for their earnings.</rest>

The King thought about this for some time. Then he asked, <rest>Why should so few men be able to acquire all the gold?</rest>

<rest>Because they know how,</rest> replied the Chancellor. <rest>You can't condemn a man for succeeding because he knows how. Nor is it fair to take away what a man has fairly earned, just to give it to people with less ability.</rest>

<rest>But why shouldn't all the people learn how to accumulate gold?</rest> demanded the King. <rest>Then they could become rich and prosperous themselves!</rest>

<rest>That is quite possible, Your Excellency. But who can teach them? Certainly not the priests, because they know nothing about making money.</rest>

The King asked, <rest>Who in our entire city knows best how to become wealthy, Chancellor?</rest>

<rest>Your question answers itself, Your Majesty. Who has amassed the greatest wealth in Babylon?</rest>

<rest>Well said, my able Chancellor. It is Arkad. He is the richest man in Babylon. Bring him to me tomorrow.</rest>

The next day, as the King commanded, Arkad appeared before him. He stood straight and energetic, even though he was seventy years old.

<rest>Arkad,</rest> spoke the King. <rest>Is it true you are the richest man in Babylon?</rest>

<arkad>So it is reported, Your Majesty, and no one disputes it.</arkad>

<rest>How did you become so wealthy?</rest>

<arkad>By taking advantage of opportunities that are available to all citizens of our good city.</arkad>

<rest>You had nothing to start with?</rest>

<arkad>Only a great desire for wealth. Besides that, nothing.</arkad>

The King continued, <rest>Arkad, our city is in a very unhappy state. A few men know how to acquire wealth and therefore monopolize it. Meanwhile, the mass of our citizens lack the knowledge of how to keep any part of the gold they receive.</rest>

<rest>I want Babylon to be the wealthiest city in the world. To do that, it must be a city of many wealthy people. Therefore, we must teach everyone how to acquire riches. Tell me, Arkad, is there a secret to acquiring wealth? Can it be taught?</rest>

<arkad>It is practical, Your Majesty. What one man knows can be taught to others.</arkad>

The King's eyes glowed. <rest>Arkad, you speak the exact words I want to hear! Will you dedicate yourself to this great cause? Will you teach your knowledge to a school for teachers? Each of them can then teach others, until there are enough trained teachers to share these truths with every worthy subject in my domain.</rest>

Arkad bowed and said, <arkad>I am your humble servant. Whatever knowledge I possess, I will gladly give for the betterment of my fellow men and the glory of my King. Let your good Chancellor arrange a class of one hundred men for me. I will teach them the seven cures that fattened my own purse, which used to be the leanest purse in all of Babylon.</arkad>

Two weeks later, following the King's command, the chosen hundred men assembled in the great hall of the Temple of Learning. They sat on colorful rugs in a semicircle. Arkad sat beside a small table. On the table, a sacred lamp smoked, sending out a strange and pleasing scent.

A student nudged his neighbor as Arkad stood up and whispered, <rest>Look, the richest man in Babylon. He is just a man, exactly like the rest of us.</rest>

Arkad began, <arkad>As a dutiful subject of our great King, I stand before you in his service. Once, I was a poor youth who greatly desired gold. Because I found the knowledge that let me acquire it, the King has asked me to share my knowledge with you.</arkad>

<arkad>I started my fortune in the humblest way. I had no advantage that you and every other citizen in Babylon don't also enjoy.</arkad>

<arkad>The first storehouse of my treasure was a simple purse. I hated its useless emptiness. I wanted it to be round and full, clinking with the sound of gold! Therefore, I looked for every possible remedy for a lean purse. I found seven.</arkad>

<arkad>To you, who are assembled before me, I will explain the seven cures for a lean purse. I recommend these to all men who desire plenty of gold. Each day for seven days, I will explain one of the seven remedies to you.</arkad>

<arkad>Listen closely to the knowledge I am about to share. Debate it with me. Discuss it among yourselves. Learn these lessons thoroughly, so that you can plant the seed of wealth in your own purses. First, each of you must wisely start building your own fortune. Only then will you be competent to teach these truths to others.</arkad>

<arkad>I will teach you in simple ways how to fatten your purses. This is the first step leading to the temple of wealth. No man can climb higher if he cannot plant his feet firmly on the first step.</arkad>

<arkad>We will now consider the first cure.</arkad>

### THE FIRST CURE:
**Start Fattening Your Purse**

Arkad addressed a thoughtful man in the second row. <arkad>My good friend, what kind of work do you do?</arkad>

The man replied, <rest>I am a scribe, and I carve records onto clay tablets.</rest>

<arkad>I earned my first coppers doing exactly that same labor,</arkad> said Arkad. <arkad>Therefore, you have the exact same opportunity to build a fortune.</arkad>

He then spoke to a red-faced man further back. <arkad>Please, tell me also, what do you do to earn your bread?</arkad>


<rest>I am a meat butcher,</rest> the man responded. <rest>I buy goats from the farmers, process them, and sell the meat to the housewives and the hides to the sandal makers.</rest>

<arkad>Because you also work and earn, you have every advantage to succeed that I possessed.</arkad>

Arkad proceeded this way to find out how each man worked to earn his living. When he finished questioning them, he said:

<arkad>Now, my students, you can see there are many trades and jobs where men can earn coins. Each way of earning is a stream of gold. By working, the worker diverts a portion of that stream into his own purse. Therefore, a stream of coins flows into each of your purses. This stream is large or small according to your ability. Isn't that true?</arkad>

They agreed that it was true. <arkad>Then,</arkad> Arkad continued, <arkad>if each of you wants to build a fortune for yourself, isn't it wise to start by using the source of wealth you have already established?</arkad>

They agreed to this as well.

Then Arkad turned to a humble man who had introduced himself as an egg merchant. <arkad>If you select one of your baskets and put ten eggs into it each morning, but take out only nine eggs each evening, what will eventually happen?</arkad>

<rest>In time, it will overflow.</rest>

<arkad>Why?</arkad>

<rest>Because each day I put in one more egg than I take out.</rest>

Arkad turned to the class with a smile. <arkad>Does anyone here have a lean purse?</arkad>

First they looked amused. Then they laughed. Finally, they waved their empty purses as a joke.

<arkad>All right,</arkad> he continued. <arkad>Now I will tell you the first remedy I learned to cure a lean purse. Do exactly as I suggested to the egg merchant. For every ten coins you place in your purse, take out only nine to use. Your purse will start to fatten at once. Its increasing weight will feel good in your hand and bring satisfaction to your soul.</arkad>

<arkad>Do not mock what I say because it is simple. The truth is always simple. I told you I would explain how I built my fortune. This was my beginning. I, too, carried a lean purse. I cursed it because it contained nothing to satisfy my desires. But when I started taking out only nine parts for every ten I put in, my purse began to fatten. Yours will do the same.</arkad>

<arkad>Now I will share a strange truth, even though I do not know the reason behind it. When I stopped spending more than nine-tenths of my earnings, I managed to get along just as well. I was not any poorer than before. Also, before long, coins came to me much more easily. Surely it is a law of the Gods that gold comes more easily to those who keep a certain part of their earnings. Likewise, gold avoids those whose purses are empty.</arkad>

<arkad>Which do you desire the most? Is it the instant gratification of your daily desires? A jewel, a bit of finery, better clothing, or more food? These things are quickly gone and forgotten. Or do you desire substantial belongings? Gold, lands, herds, merchandise, and income-producing investments? The coins you take out of your purse will buy the first list. The coins you leave inside it will bring you the second.</arkad>

<arkad>This, my students, was the first cure I discovered for my lean purse: **For every ten coins I put in, I spend only nine.** Discuss this among yourselves. If anyone can prove it untrue, tell me tomorrow when we meet again.</arkad>

THE SECOND CURE
Control your expenditures

<arkad>Some of you have asked me a very good question,</arkad> Arkad said, addressing his students on the second day. <arkad>How can a man keep one-tenth of everything he earns in his purse when all the coins he makes are not enough to cover his necessary expenses?</arkad>

<arkad>How many of you carried lean purses yesterday?</arkad> he asked.

<rest>All of us,</rest> the class answered.

<arkad>Yet, you do not all earn the same amount. Some of you earn much more than others. Some have much larger families to support. But despite these differences, all your purses were equally lean. Now I will tell you an unusual truth about human nature. It is this: what we call our necessary expenses will always grow to equal our incomes, unless we actively stop them from doing so.</arkad>

<arkad>Do not confuse your necessary expenses with your desires. Each of you, along with your families, have more desires than your earnings can pay for. Because of this, your earnings are spent to gratify these desires as far as the money will go. And yet, you still have many unsatisfied desires left over.</arkad>

<arkad>All men are burdened with more desires than they can satisfy. Do you think I can gratify every desire just because of my wealth? That is a false idea. There are limits to my time. There are limits to my strength. There are limits to the distance I can travel, limits to what I can eat, and limits to the energy I have to enjoy things.</arkad>

<arkad>I tell you that weeds will grow in a field wherever the farmer leaves space for their roots. In the same way, desires grow freely in people whenever there is a possibility of satisfying them. Your desires are endless, but the ones you can actually gratify are very few.</arkad>

<arkad>Study your normal living habits very thoughtfully. When you do, you will often find certain accepted expenses that can be wisely reduced or eliminated altogether. Let this be your motto: demand one hundred percent of appreciated value for every single coin you spend.</arkad>

<arkad>Therefore, engrave on a clay tablet every single thing you wish to spend money on. Select only the expenses that are absolutely necessary, and those that can be paid for out of nine-tenths of your income. Cross out the rest. Consider them a part of that endless multitude of desires that must go unsatisfied, and do not regret letting them go.</arkad>

<arkad>Budget your necessary expenses. Do not touch the one-tenth that is fattening your purse. Let the growth of your purse become your greatest fulfilled desire. Keep working with your budget, and keep adjusting it to help you. Make it your primary assistant in defending your growing wealth.</arkad>

At this point, one of the students wearing a robe of red and gold stood up. <rest>I am a free man,</rest> he said. <rest>I believe it is my right to enjoy the good things in life. Therefore, I rebel against the slavery of a budget that dictates exactly how much I can spend and what I can spend it on. I feel it would strip away the pleasure in my life and make me little more than a pack-ass carrying a heavy burden.</rest>

Arkad replied to him, <arkad>Who, my friend, will determine your budget?</arkad>

<rest>I would make it for myself,</rest> the protesting student responded.

<arkad>In that case, if a pack-ass were to budget his own burden, would he choose to carry jewels, rugs, and heavy bars of gold? No, he would not. He would pack hay, grain, and a bag of water for the desert trail.</arkad>

<arkad>The purpose of a budget is to help your purse fatten. It exists to help you afford your necessities, and as far as possible, your other desires. It enables you to realize your most cherished dreams by defending them against your casual, everyday wishes. Like a bright light in a dark cave, a budget reveals the leaks from your purse. It allows you to stop them and control your spending for clear, rewarding purposes.</arkad>
