"That is true."

"Then, through your self-denial, it would take you fifty years of labor to save fifty pieces of gold?"

"That would take a lifetime of labor."

"Do you think your sister would want to risk fifty years' worth of your savings from the bronze melting pot just so her husband could experiment with being a merchant?"

"Not if I explained it in those words."

"Then go to her and say: 'I have worked every day except fast days, from morning until night, for three years. I have denied myself many things my heart desired. For each year of hard labor and self-denial, I only have one piece of gold to show for it. You are my favorite sister, and I want your husband to engage in a business where he can prosper greatly. If he can submit a plan to me that seems wise and possible to my friend Mathon, then I will gladly lend him my entire savings from one year. That will give him the opportunity to prove he can succeed.' Say exactly that. If he has the soul to succeed within him, he will prove it. If he fails, he will not owe you more than he can hope to repay someday.

"I am a gold lender because I own more gold than I can use in my own trade. I want my surplus gold to work for others and earn even more gold. I do not want to risk losing it, for I have worked hard and denied myself much to secure it. Therefore, I will no longer lend any of it where I am not confident that it is safe and will be returned to me. I will also not lend it where I am not convinced that the earnings will be paid to me promptly.

"I have shared a few secrets from my token chest with you, Rodan. From them, you can understand the weakness of men. You can see their eagerness to borrow money that they have no certain way to repay. You can see how often their high hopes of making great earnings are simply false hopes. They just don't have the ability or training to fulfill them, even if they had the gold.

"You now have gold, Rodan, which you should put to work earning more gold for yourself. You are about to become a gold lender, just like me. If you safely preserve your treasure, it will produce generous earnings for you. It will be a rich source of pleasure and profit for all your days. But if you let it slip away, it will be a source of constant sorrow and regret for as long as you live.

"What do you desire most for this gold in your wallet?"

"To keep it safe."

"Wisely spoken," Mathon replied approvingly. "Your first desire is for safety. Do you think it would be truly safe from possible loss in the custody of your sister's husband?"

"I am afraid not, for he is not wise at guarding gold."

"Then do not be swayed by foolish feelings of obligation. Do not trust your treasure to just anyone. If you want to help your family or friends, find ways other than risking the loss of your treasure. Do not forget that gold slips away in unexpected ways from those who are unskilled at guarding it. You might as well waste your treasure on extravagance as let someone else lose it for you.

"What is your next desire for this treasure of yours, after safety?"

"That it earns more gold."

"Again, you speak with wisdom. It should be put to work to earn and grow larger. Gold that is wisely lent out can even double itself through earnings before a young man like you grows old. But if you risk losing the gold itself, you also risk losing everything it could have earned.

"Therefore, do not be swayed by the fantastic plans of impractical men. They think they see ways to force your gold to make unusually large earnings. Such plans are the creations of dreamers who are unskilled in the safe and dependable laws of trade. Be conservative in what you expect it to earn, so that you may keep and enjoy your treasure. To lend it out with a promise of sky-high returns is only inviting loss.

"Seek to associate yourself with men and enterprises whose success is already established. That way, your treasure can earn generously under their skillful use, while being safely guarded by their wisdom and experience.

"This way, you can avoid the misfortunes that follow most men who are entrusted with gold by the gods."

When Rodan tried to thank him for his wise advice, Mathon waved him off, saying, "The king's gift will teach you much wisdom. If you want to keep your fifty pieces of gold, you must be very discreet. Many potential uses will tempt you. Many people will offer you advice. Numerous opportunities to make large profits will be presented to you. The stories from my token box should serve as a warning. Before you let any piece of gold leave your pouch, you must be absolutely sure you have a safe way to pull it back again. If you ever want my further advice, please return. It is always gladly given.

"Before you go, read what I have carved beneath the lid of my token box. It applies equally to both the borrower and the lender:

**BETTER A LITTLE CAUTION THAN A GREAT REGRET**"
