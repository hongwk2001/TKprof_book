"What do you say, Arkad? Because you are the richest man in Babylon, many people proclaim you to be the luckiest. Do you agree with me that no man can arrive at a full measure of success until he has completely crushed the spirit of procrastination within himself?"

"It is exactly as you say," Arkad admitted. "During my long life, I have watched generation after generation. They march forward along those avenues of trade, science, and learning that lead to success in life. Opportunities came to all of these men. Some grasped theirs and moved steadily toward satisfying their deepest desires. But the majority hesitated, faltered, and fell behind."

Arkad turned to the cloth weaver. "You suggested that we debate good luck. Let us hear what you think about the subject now."

"I see good luck in a different light now," the cloth weaver said. "I had thought of it as something highly desirable that might simply happen to a man without any effort on his part. Now, I realize such magical events are not the sort of thing a person can actively attract to himself. From our discussion, I have learned that to attract good luck, it is absolutely necessary to take advantage of opportunities. Therefore, in the future, I will make every effort to make the best of the opportunities that come to me."

"You have perfectly grasped the truths brought out in our discussion," Arkad replied. "We find that good luck often follows opportunity, but it seldom comes any other way. Our merchant friend would have found great good luck if he had accepted the opportunity the good goddess presented to him. Our friend the buyer, likewise, would have enjoyed good luck if he had completed the purchase of the sheep flock and sold them at such a handsome profit.

"We pursued this discussion to find a way to entice good luck to come to us. I feel that we have successfully found the way. Both of the tales illustrated exactly how good luck follows opportunity. Here lies a powerful truth that many similar tales of good luck, whether won or lost, could never change. The truth is this: **Good luck can be enticed by accepting opportunity.**"

"Those who are eager to grasp opportunities for their own betterment will attract the interest of the good goddess. She is always anxious to help those who please her. And men of action please her the best.

"Taking action will lead you forward to the successes you deeply desire."

**MEN OF ACTION ARE FAVORED BY THE GODDESS OF GOOD LUCK**
