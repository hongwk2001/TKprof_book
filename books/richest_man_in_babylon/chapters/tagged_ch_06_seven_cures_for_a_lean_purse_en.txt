Seven Cures For a Lean Purse

The glory of Babylon endures. Through the ages, its reputation comes to us as the richest of cities, with fabulous treasures.

But it wasn't always like this. The wealth of Babylon was the result of its people's wisdom. First, they had to learn how to become wealthy.

When the Good King Sargon returned to Babylon after defeating his enemies, the Elamites, he faced a serious situation. The Royal Chancellor explained it to the King like this:

<rest>Your Majesty, we brought our people many years of great prosperity by building the great irrigation canals and the mighty temples of the Gods. But now that those works are finished, the people seem unable to support themselves.</rest>

<rest>The laborers have no jobs. The merchants have few customers. The farmers can't sell their produce. The people don't have enough gold to buy food.</rest>

The King demanded, <rest>But where has all the gold gone that we spent on these great improvements?</rest>

The Chancellor responded, <rest>I fear it has found its way into the hands of a few very rich men in our city. It filtered through the fingers of most of our people as quickly as goat's milk goes through a strainer. Now that the stream of gold has stopped flowing, most of our people have nothing to show for their earnings.</rest>

The King thought about this for some time. Then he asked, <rest>Why should so few men be able to acquire all the gold?</rest>

<rest>Because they know how,</rest> replied the Chancellor. <rest>You can't condemn a man for succeeding because he knows how. Nor is it fair to take away what a man has fairly earned, just to give it to people with less ability.</rest>

<rest>But why shouldn't all the people learn how to accumulate gold?</rest> demanded the King. <rest>Then they could become rich and prosperous themselves!</rest>

<rest>That is quite possible, Your Excellency. But who can teach them? Certainly not the priests, because they know nothing about making money.</rest>

The King asked, <rest>Who in our entire city knows best how to become wealthy, Chancellor?</rest>

<rest>Your question answers itself, Your Majesty. Who has amassed the greatest wealth in Babylon?</rest>

<rest>Well said, my able Chancellor. It is Arkad. He is the richest man in Babylon. Bring him to me tomorrow.</rest>

The next day, as the King commanded, Arkad appeared before him. He stood straight and energetic, even though he was seventy years old.

<rest>Arkad,</rest> spoke the King. <rest>Is it true you are the richest man in Babylon?</rest>

<arkad>So it is reported, Your Majesty, and no one disputes it.</arkad>

<rest>How did you become so wealthy?</rest>

<arkad>By taking advantage of opportunities that are available to all citizens of our good city.</arkad>

<rest>You had nothing to start with?</rest>

<arkad>Only a great desire for wealth. Besides that, nothing.</arkad>

The King continued, <rest>Arkad, our city is in a very unhappy state. A few men know how to acquire wealth and therefore monopolize it. Meanwhile, the mass of our citizens lack the knowledge of how to keep any part of the gold they receive.</rest>

<rest>I want Babylon to be the wealthiest city in the world. To do that, it must be a city of many wealthy people. Therefore, we must teach everyone how to acquire riches. Tell me, Arkad, is there a secret to acquiring wealth? Can it be taught?</rest>

<arkad>It is practical, Your Majesty. What one man knows can be taught to others.</arkad>

The King's eyes glowed. <rest>Arkad, you speak the exact words I want to hear! Will you dedicate yourself to this great cause? Will you teach your knowledge to a school for teachers? Each of them can then teach others, until there are enough trained teachers to share these truths with every worthy subject in my domain.</rest>

Arkad bowed and said, <arkad>I am your humble servant. Whatever knowledge I possess, I will gladly give for the betterment of my fellow men and the glory of my King. Let your good Chancellor arrange a class of one hundred men for me. I will teach them the seven cures that fattened my own purse, which used to be the leanest purse in all of Babylon.</arkad>

Two weeks later, following the King's command, the chosen hundred men assembled in the great hall of the Temple of Learning. They sat on colorful rugs in a semicircle. Arkad sat beside a small table. On the table, a sacred lamp smoked, sending out a strange and pleasing scent.

A student nudged his neighbor as Arkad stood up and whispered, <rest>Look, the richest man in Babylon. He is just a man, exactly like the rest of us.</rest>

Arkad began, <arkad>As a dutiful subject of our great King, I stand before you in his service. Once, I was a poor youth who greatly desired gold. Because I found the knowledge that let me acquire it, the King has asked me to share my knowledge with you.</arkad>

<arkad>I started my fortune in the humblest way. I had no advantage that you and every other citizen in Babylon don't also enjoy.</arkad>

<arkad>The first storehouse of my treasure was a simple purse. I hated its useless emptiness. I wanted it to be round and full, clinking with the sound of gold! Therefore, I looked for every possible remedy for a lean purse. I found seven.</arkad>

<arkad>To you, who are assembled before me, I will explain the seven cures for a lean purse. I recommend these to all men who desire plenty of gold. Each day for seven days, I will explain one of the seven remedies to you.</arkad>

<arkad>Listen closely to the knowledge I am about to share. Debate it with me. Discuss it among yourselves. Learn these lessons thoroughly, so that you can plant the seed of wealth in your own purses. First, each of you must wisely start building your own fortune. Only then will you be competent to teach these truths to others.</arkad>

<arkad>I will teach you in simple ways how to fatten your purses. This is the first step leading to the temple of wealth. No man can climb higher if he cannot plant his feet firmly on the first step.</arkad>

<arkad>We will now consider the first cure.</arkad>

### THE FIRST CURE:
**Start Fattening Your Purse**

Arkad addressed a thoughtful man in the second row. <arkad>My good friend, what kind of work do you do?</arkad>

The man replied, <rest>I am a scribe, and I carve records onto clay tablets.</rest>

<arkad>I earned my first coppers doing exactly that same labor,</arkad> said Arkad. <arkad>Therefore, you have the exact same opportunity to build a fortune.</arkad>

He then spoke to a red-faced man further back. <arkad>Please, tell me also, what do you do to earn your bread?</arkad>


<rest>I am a meat butcher,</rest> the man responded. <rest>I buy goats from the farmers, process them, and sell the meat to the housewives and the hides to the sandal makers.</rest>

<arkad>Because you also work and earn, you have every advantage to succeed that I possessed.</arkad>

Arkad proceeded this way to find out how each man worked to earn his living. When he finished questioning them, he said:

<arkad>Now, my students, you can see there are many trades and jobs where men can earn coins. Each way of earning is a stream of gold. By working, the worker diverts a portion of that stream into his own purse. Therefore, a stream of coins flows into each of your purses. This stream is large or small according to your ability. Isn't that true?</arkad>

They agreed that it was true. <arkad>Then,</arkad> Arkad continued, <arkad>if each of you wants to build a fortune for yourself, isn't it wise to start by using the source of wealth you have already established?</arkad>

They agreed to this as well.

Then Arkad turned to a humble man who had introduced himself as an egg merchant. <arkad>If you select one of your baskets and put ten eggs into it each morning, but take out only nine eggs each evening, what will eventually happen?</arkad>

<rest>In time, it will overflow.</rest>

<arkad>Why?</arkad>

<rest>Because each day I put in one more egg than I take out.</rest>

Arkad turned to the class with a smile. <arkad>Does anyone here have a lean purse?</arkad>

First they looked amused. Then they laughed. Finally, they waved their empty purses as a joke.

<arkad>All right,</arkad> he continued. <arkad>Now I will tell you the first remedy I learned to cure a lean purse. Do exactly as I suggested to the egg merchant. For every ten coins you place in your purse, take out only nine to use. Your purse will start to fatten at once. Its increasing weight will feel good in your hand and bring satisfaction to your soul.</arkad>

<arkad>Do not mock what I say because it is simple. The truth is always simple. I told you I would explain how I built my fortune. This was my beginning. I, too, carried a lean purse. I cursed it because it contained nothing to satisfy my desires. But when I started taking out only nine parts for every ten I put in, my purse began to fatten. Yours will do the same.</arkad>

<arkad>Now I will share a strange truth, even though I do not know the reason behind it. When I stopped spending more than nine-tenths of my earnings, I managed to get along just as well. I was not any poorer than before. Also, before long, coins came to me much more easily. Surely it is a law of the Gods that gold comes more easily to those who keep a certain part of their earnings. Likewise, gold avoids those whose purses are empty.</arkad>

<arkad>Which do you desire the most? Is it the instant gratification of your daily desires? A jewel, a bit of finery, better clothing, or more food? These things are quickly gone and forgotten. Or do you desire substantial belongings? Gold, lands, herds, merchandise, and income-producing investments? The coins you take out of your purse will buy the first list. The coins you leave inside it will bring you the second.</arkad>

<arkad>This, my students, was the first cure I discovered for my lean purse: **For every ten coins I put in, I spend only nine.** Discuss this among yourselves. If anyone can prove it untrue, tell me tomorrow when we meet again.</arkad>

THE SECOND CURE
Control your expenditures

<arkad>Some of you have asked me a very good question,</arkad> Arkad said, addressing his students on the second day. <arkad>How can a man keep one-tenth of everything he earns in his purse when all the coins he makes are not enough to cover his necessary expenses?</arkad>

<arkad>How many of you carried lean purses yesterday?</arkad> he asked.

<rest>All of us,</rest> the class answered.

<arkad>Yet, you do not all earn the same amount. Some of you earn much more than others. Some have much larger families to support. But despite these differences, all your purses were equally lean. Now I will tell you an unusual truth about human nature. It is this: what we call our necessary expenses will always grow to equal our incomes, unless we actively stop them from doing so.</arkad>

<arkad>Do not confuse your necessary expenses with your desires. Each of you, along with your families, have more desires than your earnings can pay for. Because of this, your earnings are spent to gratify these desires as far as the money will go. And yet, you still have many unsatisfied desires left over.</arkad>

<arkad>All men are burdened with more desires than they can satisfy. Do you think I can gratify every desire just because of my wealth? That is a false idea. There are limits to my time. There are limits to my strength. There are limits to the distance I can travel, limits to what I can eat, and limits to the energy I have to enjoy things.</arkad>

<arkad>I tell you that weeds will grow in a field wherever the farmer leaves space for their roots. In the same way, desires grow freely in people whenever there is a possibility of satisfying them. Your desires are endless, but the ones you can actually gratify are very few.</arkad>

<arkad>Study your normal living habits very thoughtfully. When you do, you will often find certain accepted expenses that can be wisely reduced or eliminated altogether. Let this be your motto: demand one hundred percent of appreciated value for every single coin you spend.</arkad>

<arkad>Therefore, engrave on a clay tablet every single thing you wish to spend money on. Select only the expenses that are absolutely necessary, and those that can be paid for out of nine-tenths of your income. Cross out the rest. Consider them a part of that endless multitude of desires that must go unsatisfied, and do not regret letting them go.</arkad>

<arkad>Budget your necessary expenses. Do not touch the one-tenth that is fattening your purse. Let the growth of your purse become your greatest fulfilled desire. Keep working with your budget, and keep adjusting it to help you. Make it your primary assistant in defending your growing wealth.</arkad>

At this point, one of the students wearing a robe of red and gold stood up. <rest>I am a free man,</rest> he said. <rest>I believe it is my right to enjoy the good things in life. Therefore, I rebel against the slavery of a budget that dictates exactly how much I can spend and what I can spend it on. I feel it would strip away the pleasure in my life and make me little more than a pack-ass carrying a heavy burden.</rest>

Arkad replied to him, <arkad>Who, my friend, will determine your budget?</arkad>

<rest>I would make it for myself,</rest> the protesting student responded.

<arkad>In that case, if a pack-ass were to budget his own burden, would he choose to carry jewels, rugs, and heavy bars of gold? No, he would not. He would pack hay, grain, and a bag of water for the desert trail.</arkad>

<arkad>The purpose of a budget is to help your purse fatten. It exists to help you afford your necessities, and as far as possible, your other desires. It enables you to realize your most cherished dreams by defending them against your casual, everyday wishes. Like a bright light in a dark cave, a budget reveals the leaks from your purse. It allows you to stop them and control your spending for clear, rewarding purposes.</arkad>

<arkad>This, then, is the second cure for a lean purse: **Budget your expenses so that you have coins to pay for your necessities, to pay for your enjoyments, and to gratify your worthwhile desires without spending more than nine-tenths of your earnings.**</arkad>


THE THIRD CURE 
Make Your Gold Multiply 

On the third day, Arkad addressed his class.

<arkad>Look at your lean purse—it is finally getting fat! You have disciplined yourself to keep one-tenth of everything you earn. You have controlled your spending to protect your growing treasure. Now, we will talk about how to put that treasure to work so it can grow even more. Keeping gold in a purse feels good, and it might satisfy a miser, but it earns absolutely nothing. The gold we save from our earnings is just the beginning. The money that gold earns for us is what will actually build our fortunes.</arkad>

<arkad>So, how do we put our gold to work? My very first investment was a disaster, and I lost everything. I will tell you that story later. But my first profitable investment was a loan I made to a shield maker named Aggar. Every year, he bought huge shipments of bronze that came from across the sea to use in his business. Because he didn't have enough capital to pay the merchants upfront, he would borrow from people who had extra coins. Aggar was an honorable man. When he sold his shields, he always paid back the loan, along with a generous rental fee for using the money.</arkad>

<arkad>Every time I loaned him money, I also loaned back the rental fee he had just paid me. Because of this, my capital increased, and the earnings from it increased too. It was extremely satisfying to watch those growing sums return to my purse.</arkad>

<arkad>I tell you, my students, a person's wealth is not the coins they carry in their purse. Wealth is the income they build. It is the golden stream that constantly flows into their purse and keeps it bulging. That is what everyone wants. That is what each of you wants: an income that keeps coming in, whether you are working or traveling.</arkad>

<arkad>I have built a massive income. It is so large that people call me a very rich man. Those loans to Aggar were my first real lesson in profitable investing. I gained wisdom from that experience, and as my capital grew, I expanded my loans and investments. At first, my wealth came from just a few places. Later on, it flowed from many different sources. It became a golden stream pouring into my purse, ready for me to use wisely however I chose.</arkad>

<arkad>From my humble beginnings, I had raised an army of golden slaves. Each one worked hard and earned me even more gold. And as they worked for me, their children worked for me too, and then their children's children. Eventually, the income from all their combined efforts became massive.</arkad>

<arkad>Gold grows incredibly fast when it earns a reasonable return, as you will see from this story. A farmer had his first son. He took ten pieces of silver to a money lender and asked him to hold it in an account for the boy until he turned twenty. The money lender agreed. He promised that the rental fee would be one-fourth of the silver's value every four years. Because the farmer wanted this money to be a dedicated fund for his son, he asked the money lender to add the rental fee directly to the principal.</arkad>

<arkad>When the boy turned twenty, the farmer went back to the money lender to ask about the silver. The money lender explained that because the money had been earning compound interest, the original ten pieces of silver had grown into thirty and a half pieces.</arkad>

<arkad>The farmer was thrilled. Since his son didn't need the coins right then, he left them with the money lender. When the son turned fifty, the father had already passed away. But the money lender settled the account and paid the son a total of one hundred and sixty-seven pieces of silver.</arkad>

<arkad>In fifty years, that single investment had multiplied almost seventeen times!</arkad>

<arkad>This is the third cure for a lean purse: Put every single coin to work. Let it reproduce itself, just like flocks in the fields. Let it build your income into a massive stream of wealth that flows constantly into your purse.</arkad>



THE FOURTH CURE 
Guard Your Treasures From Loss 

On the fourth day, Arkad spoke to his class again.

<arkad>Misfortune loves a shiny target. If you don't guard the gold in your purse firmly, it will be lost. That is why it is smart to start by securing small amounts of money. You must learn to protect the small sums before the Gods will ever trust you with larger ones.</arkad>

<arkad>Every person with gold gets tempted by opportunities. It often seems like you could make huge amounts of money by investing in projects that sound perfectly reasonable. Sometimes, friends and relatives eagerly jump into these investments and urge you to follow them.</arkad>

<arkad>The first sound principle of investing is the security of your principal. Is it really smart to chase after huge earnings if you might lose your original investment? I say no. The penalty for taking careless risks is almost always losing your money. Before you hand over your treasure, study the situation carefully. Make absolutely sure you can get your money back safely. Don't let your own romantic dreams of getting rich quick mislead you.</arkad>

<arkad>Before you loan money to anyone, make certain they actually have the ability to pay you back. Check their reputation for keeping promises. Otherwise, you might just be giving them your hard-earned treasure as a gift.</arkad>

<arkad>Before you put your money into any new field, familiarize yourself with all the dangers and risks involved.</arkad>

<arkad>My own first investment was a total tragedy at the time. I took an entire year's worth of guarded savings and trusted it to a brickmaker named Azmur. He was traveling across the far seas to Tyre, and he agreed to buy rare Phoenician jewels for me. We planned to sell them when he got back and split the profits. But the Phoenicians were scoundrels, and they sold him worthless bits of glass. My treasure was entirely lost. Today, my experience would instantly show me how foolish it is to trust a brickmaker to buy jewels!</arkad>

<arkad>So, I give you this advice based on my own painful experiences: do not be too confident in your own wisdom. When you are dealing with the pitfalls of investing, it is much better to consult people who actually have experience handling money for profit. That kind of advice is freely given if you just ask for it. It can easily be worth just as much as the gold you are planning to invest. In fact, if it saves you from a massive loss, that is its exact value.</arkad>

<arkad>This is the fourth cure for a lean purse, and it is incredibly important because it stops your purse from being emptied once you finally fill it. Guard your treasure from loss. Only invest where your principal is perfectly safe, where you can reclaim it if you need to, and where you are guaranteed to collect a fair return. Consult with wise people. Get advice from those who are experienced in managing gold profitably. Let their wisdom protect your treasure from unsafe investments.</arkad>



THE FIFTH CURE 
Make Your Home a Profitable Investment 

On the fifth day, Arkad taught his next lesson.

<arkad>Suppose a man sets aside nine-tenths of his earnings to live on and enjoy life. If he can take any part of that nine-tenths and turn it into a profitable investment without hurting his well-being, his wealth will grow so much faster.</arkad>

<arkad>Far too many men in Babylon raise their families in terrible conditions. They pay demanding landlords high rents for cramped rooms. In these places, their wives don't have a single spot of dirt to grow the flowers that make a woman's heart happy. Their children have nowhere to play their games except in the dirty alleys.</arkad>

<arkad>No family can fully enjoy life unless they have their own plot of ground. Children need a place to play in the clean earth. A wife needs space to grow beautiful blossoms and rich herbs to feed her family.</arkad>

<arkad>It brings pure joy to a man's heart to eat figs from his own trees and grapes from his own vines. Owning your own home—and making it a place you are proud to care for—puts confidence in your heart. It puts greater energy behind everything you do. Because of this, I strongly recommend that every person own the roof that shelters them and their family.</arkad>

<arkad>And it is not impossible for any well-intentioned person to own a home. Hasn't our great king extended the walls of Babylon so far that there is plenty of unused land inside? That land can be bought for incredibly reasonable prices.</arkad>

<arkad>I also tell you, my students, that money lenders are happy to work with people who want homes and land for their families. If you can show that you have saved a reasonable portion of the money yourself, you can easily borrow the rest to pay the brickmakers and builders. It is a highly commendable purpose.</arkad>

<arkad>Once your house is built, you simply pay the money lender with the exact same regularity that you used to pay the landlord. Because every payment reduces your debt to the money lender, it will only take a few years to pay off the loan completely.</arkad>

<arkad>Then your heart will be happy, because you will actually own a valuable piece of property. From then on, your only cost will be the king's taxes.</arkad>

<arkad>Your good wife will gladly go to the river more often to wash your clothes, because she knows that on her way back, she can bring a goatskin of water to pour on her own growing garden.</arkad>

<arkad>So many blessings come to the person who owns their own house. It greatly reduces your cost of living, which frees up more of your earnings for life's pleasures and satisfying your desires. This is the fifth cure for a lean purse: Own your own home.</arkad>



THE SIXTH CURE 
Ensure a Future Income 

On the sixth day, Arkad addressed his class once more.

<arkad>Every person's life moves naturally from childhood to old age. This is the path of life, and no one can step off it unless the Gods call them away prematurely. Because of this, I say that you must prepare a suitable income for the future. You must plan for the days when you are no longer young. You must also make preparations for your family, in case you are no longer there to comfort and support them. This lesson will teach you how to keep a full purse when time has made you less able to work and learn.</arkad>

<arkad>A person who understands the laws of wealth and builds a growing surplus must think about those future days. They should set up certain investments and provisions that will last safely for many years. These investments must be ready and available when that anticipated time finally arrives.</arkad>


<arkad>There are many ways a person can safely provide for their future. They could find a hiding place and bury a secret treasure. But no matter how skillfully it is hidden, thieves might still find and steal it. For this reason, I do not recommend this plan.</arkad>

<arkad>A person might buy houses or lands for this purpose. If they are chosen wisely for their future usefulness and value, they become permanent assets. Their earnings or future sale will provide well for this purpose.</arkad>

<arkad>A person might loan a small sum to the money lender and add to it at regular periods. The interest that the money lender adds will greatly increase its value. I know a sandal maker named Ansan. He explained to me not long ago that every week for eight years, he had deposited two pieces of silver with his money lender. The money lender recently gave him an accounting, and Ansan rejoiced greatly. The total of his small deposits, along with the interest at the customary rate of one-fourth their value every four years, had grown to a thousand and forty pieces of silver.</arkad>

<arkad>I gladly encouraged him further. I used my knowledge of numbers to show him that in twelve more years, if he kept depositing just two pieces of silver each week, the money lender would owe him four thousand pieces of silver. This would be a worthy income for the rest of his life.</arkad>

<arkad>Surely, when such a small regular payment produces such profitable results, no one can afford not to secure a treasure for their old age and the protection of their family. This is true no matter how prosperous their business and investments may currently be.</arkad>

<arkad>I wish I could say more about this. I believe that someday, wise-thinking people will invent a plan to insure against death. Under this plan, many people would regularly pay in a tiny sum, and the combined amount would provide a handsome sum for the family of each member who passes away. I see this as something desirable, and I would highly recommend it.</arkad>

<arkad>But today, this is not possible because it must operate beyond the life of any single person or partnership. It must be as stable as the King's throne. I feel that someday such a plan will come to pass and be a great blessing to many people. Even the first small payment would make a snug fortune available for a member's family if they passed on.</arkad>

<arkad>But we live in our own time, not in the days to come. We must take advantage of the means and ways available to accomplish our goals. Therefore, I recommend to everyone that they use wise and well-thought-out methods to protect against a lean purse in their later years. A lean purse is a terrible tragedy for someone who is no longer able to earn, or for a family that has lost its provider.</arkad>

<arkad>This, then, is the sixth cure for a lean purse: Provide in advance for the needs of your growing age and the protection of your family.</arkad>

THE SEVENTH CURE
Increase your ability to earn

<arkad>Today I will speak to you, my students, about one of the most vital remedies for a lean purse,</arkad> Arkad addressed his class on the seventh day. <arkad>However, I will not talk about gold, but about yourselves. I will talk about the men beneath the brightly colored robes who sit before me. I will talk to you about the things within the minds and lives of people that work for or against their success.</arkad>

<arkad>Not long ago, a young man came to me seeking to borrow money. When I asked him why he needed it, he complained that his earnings were not enough to pay his expenses. I explained to him that in this case, he was a bad customer for the money lender. He had no extra earning capacity to repay the loan.</arkad>

<arkad>I told him, 'What you need, young man, is to earn more coins. What are you doing to increase your capacity to earn?'</arkad>

<arkad>'All that I can do,' he replied. 'Six times in the last two months I have approached my master to ask for a raise, but without success. No one can go more often than that.'</arkad>

<arkad>We might smile at his simplicity, yet he did possess one of the vital requirements to increase his earnings. Within him was a strong desire to earn more, which is a proper and commendable desire.</arkad>

<arkad>Desire must come before accomplishment. Your desires must be strong and definite. General desires are nothing more than weak longings. For a person to simply wish to be rich has little purpose. But for a person to desire five pieces of gold is a tangible goal that they can push toward fulfillment. Once they have backed their desire for five pieces of gold with a strong purpose to secure it, they can then find similar ways to obtain ten pieces, then twenty pieces, and later a thousand pieces. Behold, they have become wealthy. By learning to secure one definite small desire, they have trained themselves to secure a larger one. This is the process by which wealth is accumulated. It starts with small sums, then grows to larger ones as a person learns and becomes more capable.</arkad>

<arkad>Desires must be simple and definite. They defeat their own purpose if they are too many, too confusing, or beyond a person's training to accomplish.</arkad>

<arkad>As a person perfects themselves in their profession, their ability to earn increases. Back when I was a humble scribe carving upon clay tablets for a few copper coins each day, I noticed that other workers did more than I did and were paid more. Therefore, I determined that I would not be outperformed by anyone. It did not take long for me to discover the reason for their greater success. I put more interest into my work, concentrated harder on my tasks, and persisted in my efforts. Soon, few could carve more tablets in a day than I could. With reasonable promptness, my increased skill was rewarded. It was not necessary for me to go to my master six times to request recognition.</arkad>

<arkad>The more wisdom we possess, the more we can earn. The person who seeks to learn more about their craft will be richly rewarded. If they are an artisan, they should seek to learn the methods and tools of the most skillful in their trade. If they work in law or medicine, they should consult and exchange knowledge with others in their profession. If they are a merchant, they should continually seek better goods that can be bought at lower prices.</arkad>

<arkad>Human affairs always change and improve because sharp-minded people seek greater skill to better serve those who rely on them. Therefore, I urge everyone to be at the forefront of progress and not to stand still, or else they will be left behind. Many things make a person's life rich with profitable experiences. If a person respects themselves, they must do the following things:</arkad>

<arkad>They must pay their debts as promptly as possible, and not buy what they cannot afford to pay for.</arkad>

<arkad>They must take care of their family so that their loved ones may think and speak well of them.</arkad>

<arkad>They must create a written will so that if the Gods call them away, their property will be divided properly and honorably.</arkad>

<arkad>They must have compassion for those who are injured and struck by misfortune, and help them within reasonable limits. They must also do thoughtful deeds for those dear to them.</arkad>

<arkad>Thus, the seventh and final remedy for a lean purse is to cultivate your own powers. Study and become wiser, become more skillful, and act in a way that makes you respect yourself. By doing this, you will gain the self-confidence needed to achieve your carefully considered desires.</arkad>

<arkad>These, then, are the seven cures for a lean purse. Out of the experience of a long and successful life, I urge all who desire wealth to follow them. There is more gold in Babylon, my students, than you could ever dream of. There is an abundance for everyone.</arkad>

<arkad>Go forth and practice these truths so that you may prosper and grow wealthy, as is your right.</arkad>

<arkad>Go forth and teach these truths so that every honorable subject of his majesty may also share generously in the ample wealth of our beloved city.</arkad>
