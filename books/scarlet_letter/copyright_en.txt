A Note on This Modernized Edition

This edition of The Scarlet Letter was adapted to make Hawthorne's masterpiece accessible to language learners, students, and casual readers. We applied the following modernizing guidelines throughout:

* Audiobook and Listening Optimization: Every sentence was structured for natural spoken flow. Subjects come first, and complex poetic inversions were removed so the narrative is easy to follow when listening.

* Linear Idea Progression: Complex or winding thoughts were broken into clear, logical steps. This helps the reader follow the action and emotion of Hester's story without losing the thread of the sentence.

* Clean Punctuation: Visual clutter such as em-dashes and parenthetical hyphens was removed. They were replaced with smooth commas or separate sentences.

* Aesthetic Restoration: All 58 original illustrations, decorative ornaments, and initial drop caps from the 1878 edition were preserved and digitally colorized to bring the visual beauty of the era to life.

Modernization Sample (Original vs. Modernized Casual)

* **Original (Victorian Prose):** "A throng of bearded men, in sad-colored garments and gray, steeple-crowned hats, intermixed with women, some wearing hoods, and others bareheaded, was assembled in front of a wooden edifice, the door of which was heavily timbered with oak, and studded with iron spikes."
* **Modernized Edition:** "A crowd of bearded men in dark, gloomy clothes and gray, pointed hats, mixed with women, some wearing hoods and others bareheaded, gathered in front of a wooden building. The building's door was made of heavy oak timber and covered with iron spikes."
