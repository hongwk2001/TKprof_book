Chapter Thirteen: The Battle in the Hall

Grendel marched from the moors beneath the misty cliffs, carrying God's anger. The wicked monster planned to capture and kill someone in the great hall. He walked under the clouds until he saw the feast-hall, the gold-decorated home of men, shining in the dark. This was not the first time he had visited Hrothgar's home, but never in his life, before or since, did he encounter worse luck or a tougher guard!

The joyless creature reached the building. The door, reinforced with forged iron bands, burst open the moment he touched it. Driven by evil rage, he tore open the entrance of the hall. Instantly, the demon stepped onto the decorated floor, moving angrily. A horrible light, looking like a flame, gleamed from his eyes. He saw many heroes in the hall, a sleeping band of relatives and warriors gathered together. Grendel laughed in his heart, planning to take the lives of every single one of them before daybreak, expecting a massive feast.

But fate would not allow Grendel to eat anyone else after that night. Beowulf, the nephew of Hygelac, watched carefully to see how the monster would attack. Grendel did not wait. For his first move, he quickly seized a sleeping warrior, tore him apart, bit through his bones, drank the blood from his veins, and swallowed huge bites. Soon, he had completely devoured the dead man, even his hands and feet, leaving nothing behind.

Stepping closer, Grendel reached out his hand to seize Beowulf on his bed. But as the demon reached for him, Beowulf grabbed Grendel's hand and propped himself up on his arm, locking him in a tight grip. As soon as the monster felt Beowulf's grip, he realized he had never met a man with a stronger grip in all the world! Terrified, Grendel wanted to escape, but he could not break free. He only wanted to flee into the darkness and hide with other devils. His experience in Heorot was unlike anything he had ever faced before.
