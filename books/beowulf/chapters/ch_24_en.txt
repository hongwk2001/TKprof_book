Chapter Twenty-Four: The Death of Aeschere and the Swamp of Monsters

Hrothgar, the Danish king, answered: "Do not ask about joy! Sorrow has returned to our people, and my heart is heavy. Aeschere is dead. He was the older brother of Yrmenlaf, who is my trusted counselor. Aeschere was my brother-in-arms in battle, a man who fought side by side with me. We defended each other as helmets shattered around us. Aeschere was everything a noble warrior should be, a true and loyal companion!

A restless, murderous monster has killed him in Heorot. I do not know where she fled, proud of her kill and celebrating her feast. She came to avenge the battle from yesterday, when you crushed Grendel with your bare hands. For too long, Grendel destroyed my people and ruined my land. He finally fell in battle, losing his life. Now another mighty monster has come to avenge her son. She has carried her revenge very far, bringing heavy sorrow to my noblemen who mourn their fallen lord. The loyal hand that once fulfilled all your wishes now lies cold and lifeless.

My people have often told me stories of two giant stalkers roaming the swamps. One of them looked like a woman. The other miserable shape walked the path of exile in the form of a man, though he was larger than any human. In the old days, my people named him Grendel. They know nothing of his father, or whether any other dark spirits were born before him.

They live in a desolate land of wolf-haunted slopes, windy cliffs, and dangerous swamp paths. There, a mountain stream plunges down beneath the dark cliffs, flowing deep underground. The lake lies only a few miles from here. Frost-covered trees hang over the water, and thick woods shadow the pool. Every night, a terrifying wonder occurs there, as fire burns upon the water. No man alive is wise enough to know how deep the lake goes.

Even if a wild stag with strong horns is chased from afar by hunting hounds, the beast will die on the bank rather than jump into those waters. It is a dreadful and cursed place. When the wind stirs up terrible storms, dark waves rise to the clouds until the air grows gloomy and the heavens weep.

Once again, we must rely on your help. You do not yet know this dangerous region, nor do you know the lair where this creature hides. Seek it if you dare! If you return safely from this battle, I will reward you with ancient treasures and twisted gold, just as I did before."
