Chapter Fifteen: The Defeat of Grendel

Grendel's death was destined to be miserable, and his evil spirit was bound for hell. The monster, who had committed so many crimes against mankind and fought against God, discovered that his body would no longer obey him. Beowulf held him tightly by the hand, and each hated the other with a deadly passion.

The terrible monster suffered a horrific wound. A giant, gaping tear ripped open his shoulder, his sinews tore apart, and his bones cracked under the pressure. Beowulf was granted supreme glory in the battle. Bleeding to death, Grendel was forced to flee down the swamp slopes, heading for his joyless lair. He knew for certain that his life had come to an end, and that his final hour had arrived.

After that bloody battle, the desires of the Danes were fulfilled. The brave hero who had traveled from afar had saved Hrothgar's hall and rescued it from ruin. Beowulf rejoiced in his night of work and his brave achievements. The Geatish leader had fulfilled his boast to the Danes, completely relieving all the distress and sorrow they had suffered for so long.

As proof of his victory, Beowulf hung Grendel's hand, arm, and shoulder high under the wide roof of the hall. The monster's entire claw was on display for everyone to see!
