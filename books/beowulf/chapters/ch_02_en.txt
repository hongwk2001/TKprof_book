Chapter Two: The Terror of Grendel and the Sorrow of King Hrothgar

One night, Grendel went to the great hall to see what the Danish warriors did after a long evening of drinking. He found the noble warriors fast asleep, completely unaware of any danger or human misery. The fierce and hungry demon attacked immediately. He was savage and cruel, dragging thirty warriors out of their beds. He rushed back to his dark lair in the swamp, celebrating his loot and carrying the slaughtered bodies with him.

At dawn, the remaining warriors woke up and realized what Grendel had done. The joy of the feast was replaced by loud crying and deep grief. Hrothgar, their famous king, sat in deep misery. The strong leader suffered for his lost men when he saw the tracks left by the accursed demon. The struggle was too heavy, too disgusting, and went on for too long.

Only one night passed before Grendel returned to commit more murders. He felt no remorse for his crimes because he was too set in his evil ways. Soon, it was easy to find men who preferred to sleep elsewhere, far from the hall. Once the monster's hatred was made clear to everyone, anyone who survived kept themselves as far away from Heorot as possible to stay alive.

So Grendel ruled, fighting unjustly against everyone, until the beautiful hall stood empty. The Danish king suffered twelve long years of grief and sorrow. Soon, the sad story spread across the world. People sang songs about how Grendel fought Hrothgar, waging a hateful war season after season. The monster refused to make peace with the Danes, stop his attacks, or accept payments to end the feud. No one could expect a fair settlement from his hands. The terrible monster was like a dark shadow of death, hunting both young and old. Night after night, he ruled the misty moors, and no one knew where such demons wandered in the dark.

The enemy of mankind committed many terrible crimes and humiliations. During the dark nights, he lived inside the beautiful hall of Heorot. However, he was never allowed to touch the king's sacred throne, nor could he feel God's love, because he was completely cursed. This was a time of deep misery and heartbreak for Hrothgar. His nobles gathered in meetings, trying to figure out how to protect themselves. In their desperation, they even prayed at pagan shrines, begging their old gods to save them. That was their custom. They turned their thoughts to hell because they did not know the true Creator, the Judge of all deeds, or how to praise the Lord of Heaven.
