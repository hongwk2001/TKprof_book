Chapter Twenty-Three: The Revenge of Grendel's Mother

The Danish warriors fell asleep in Heorot, believing the danger had finally passed. But they were wrong, and one of them was about to pay a heavy price for his rest. This kind of tragedy had happened often in the past when Grendel ruled the hall, spreading terror until death ended his crimes. Now, it soon became clear to everyone that an avenger survived the monster. Grendel was dead, but his mother was still alive, mourning her son's death and plotting her revenge.

Grendel's mother was a monstrous creature who brooded over her misery in the dark. She was doomed to live in the freezing waters and cold streams ever since Cain killed his brother with a sword. Because of that first murder, Cain fled from the joys of men and lived in the wasteland as an outlaw. Many demons and monsters descended from Cain, and Grendel was one of them. But Grendel had met Beowulf, who was waiting awake for battle in the feast-hall. The monster gripped him, but Beowulf remembered the mighty strength that God had given him. He trusted the Almighty for help and comfort, using his great strength to defeat the demon. After the fierce struggle, Grendel fled to his swamp, dying and defeated.

Now his mother, filled with grief and rage, decided to seek revenge for her son's death. She came to Heorot, where the Danish warriors slept peacefully, unaware of the new threat. The men faced a sudden disaster when she broke into the hall. Her attack was slightly less terrifying than Grendel's, just as a woman's strength in battle is less than a man's when a forged sword cuts through a helmet, but she was still a deadly enemy.

The warriors woke up in a panic, drawing their sharp swords and raising their heavy shields. No one had time to put on a helmet or chain-mail armor because the terror struck so quickly. The monster was in a desperate hurry to escape and save her life once she realized she was discovered. She quickly grabbed one of the noble warriors in her tight grip and fled back to the swamp.

This victim was Hrothgar's most beloved companion, a famous shield-warrior whom she killed in his bed. Beowulf was not in the hall when this happened, for the Danes had given the famous Geat separate quarters after the celebratory feast. A loud outcry rose from Heorot as the warriors discovered the horror. Grendel's mother had also stolen the bloody arm of her son, which had been hanging from the rafters. Deep sorrow returned to the hall. It was a tragic trade, as both sides paid with the lives of their dearest friends.

The old king was heartbroken when he learned his dearest advisor was dead. He sent for Beowulf immediately, needing his help once more. At daybreak, the victorious champion walked with his followers to the king's chambers. The wooden floor resounded under their steps. Beowulf greeted the wise king and asked if he had rested well, not yet knowing about the sudden attack in the night.
