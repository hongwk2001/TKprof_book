Chapter One: The Great Hall, Heorot and the Monster, Grendel

For many years, Beow the Dane ruled the Danes as their beloved king, taking the throne after his father Scyld passed away. In time, Beow had a son named Half-Dane, who grew to be a fierce warrior and a wise ruler. Half-Dane governed the happy kingdom for the rest of his life. He had four children: Heorogar, Hrothgar, Halga the Good, and a daughter who married King Onela of Sweden.

Hrothgar became a very successful and glorious war leader. His loyal followers obeyed him eagerly, and his band of young warriors grew into a mighty army. Eventually, Hrothgar decided to build a magnificent feast-hall. He wanted it to be grander and more beautiful than any building ever built by man. In this hall, he planned to share all the gifts God had given him with his people, both young and old, keeping only the public land and the lives of his men for himself. He sent orders to tribes all over the world to help decorate this great building. Soon, the massive hall was finished, and Hrothgar named it Heorot. True to his word, the king handed out rings and treasures to his men at the feasts. The hall was high and beautiful, though it was destined to be destroyed by fire years later when a deadly family feud broke out.

However, a powerful demon living in the dark nearby suffered great pain. Every day, the monster had to listen to the loud sounds of joy and laughter coming from the hall. He heard the music of the harp and the clear songs of the poets. The poets sang about how the Almighty created the earth, making the bright plains and surrounding them with water. They sang about how God set the sun and moon to light the land for all living things. The king's men lived in happiness until a fiend from hell began to attack them. This grim monster was named Grendel. He was a hateful creature who wandered the borders of the kingdom, ruling the lonely moors, swamps, and wilderness. Grendel lived among monsters because God had cursed him as a descendant of Cain. When Cain murdered Abel, God banished him from mankind. From Cain came all the monsters and evil spirits of the world, including giants, dark spirits, and demons who fought against God until he finally destroyed them.
