Chapter Thirty-Two: The Departure and Hrothgar's Tears

Beowulf, the son of Ecgtheow, spoke: "Now we Seafarers, having come from afar, wish to say that we are eager to return to our lord, King Hygelac. We have been entertained here splendidly, just as we desired, and you have treated us with great kindness. If I can ever earn any more of your affection on earth, noble ruler, I am ready to fight for you at once. If I hear across the sea that neighboring nations threaten you with war, as enemies have done in the past, I will bring a thousand noble warriors to help you.

I know that King Hygelac, though he is young, will support me in word and deed. He will allow me to defend you and bring a forest of spears to your aid when you need brave warriors. Furthermore, if your son Hrethric decides to visit the Geatish courts, he will find many friends there. A brave man is always welcomed in far-off lands."

Hrothgar answered: "The wise Lord put those words into your mind! I have never heard a young man speak so wisely. You are strong in Might, mature in mind, and wise in speech. I believe that if sickness, a spear, or a sword should ever take the life of King Hygelac, the Geats would find no better man to choose as their king and guardian of their treasures than you, if you are willing to rule.

Your character pleases me more the longer I know you, beloved Beowulf. You have ensured that there will be peace between the Geats and the Danes, ending the conflicts and hostilities they suffered in the past. As long as I rule this wide kingdom, we will share our treasures. Our curved prow ships will carry gifts and tokens of friendship across the wide ocean. I know our peoples will remain loyal to their friends and fierce to their enemies, living in the honorable ways of old."

Then Hrothgar presented Beowulf with twelve more treasures inside the feast-hall. He wished him a safe journey back to his people and urged him to return one day. The old king kissed the great hero and clasped him around the neck, weeping as he did so. The wise king had a strong feeling that they would never see each other again. Beowulf was so dear to him that Hrothgar could not restrain his sorrow, and a deep, secret longing for the hero filled his heart as the Geat walked away.
