Chapter Seven: Beowulf's Pledge to Fight Grendel

Beowulf spoke, his chain-mail armor gleaming like a net hand-woven by a skilled blacksmith: "Hail to you, Hrothgar! I am King Hygelac's nephew and warrior. I have already performed many glorious deeds in my youth. News of Grendel reached me in my homeland. Seafarers say that this great hall stands empty and useless as soon as the evening light fades beneath the sky.

My wise and noble people advised me to seek you out, King Hrothgar, because they knew the strength of my Might. They watched me return from battles stained with the blood of my enemies. They saw me capture five giants, destroy their clan, and hunt sea-monsters in the ocean at night. I endured terrible hardships to avenge my people, crushing their enemies. Now, I will face the monster Grendel alone.

Therefore, I ask you one favor, king of the Danes. Do not refuse me now that I have traveled so far. Allow me and my men alone to clear out your hall of Heorot. I hear that the monster is so reckless that he does not even use weapons. To make my uncle, King Hygelac, proud, I refuse to use a sword or shield. Instead, I will fight the fiend with my bare hands, struggling for life face-to-face. Whoever dies must accept the judgment of the Lord.

If the monster wins, I expect he will fearlessly eat my Geatish warriors in this hall, just as he has devoured your own brave men. If I die, you will not need to bury me. The monster will carry my blood-stained body away to the moors. The lonely beast will eat it ruthlessly and stain his lair in the swamp. You will not have to worry about my funeral. 

But if I fall in battle, send my chain-mail armor back to King Hygelac. It is a family treasure from King Hrethel, crafted by the legendary blacksmith Weland. Fate will always go as it must!"
