Chapter Fourteen: The Victory and Grendel's Torn Arm

Then, Beowulf remembered his boast from the evening. He stood upright and grabbed the monster tightly. Grendel's fingers cracked under the pressure. The giant tried to pull away, but the hero stepped closer, keeping his grip tight. The monster wanted to escape and flee to his swamp home, knowing his fingers were caught in a terrifying grasp. It was a disastrous journey the destroyer had made to Heorot!

The hall echoed with the noise of the struggle. Complete panic fell upon all the Danes and their brave warriors. Both of the fierce combatants were furious, and the building shook. It was a miracle that the beautiful feast-hall withstood the fight and did not collapse to the ground. But it was firmly reinforced inside and out with iron bands, forged with incredible skill. Many gold-decorated benches were torn from the floor during the struggle. The wise Danes had never thought that anyone could break the building apart or destroy it, unless it was swallowed by fire.

A strange and terrifying sound rose up again and again. Panic struck the Danes, hitting everyone who heard the wailing through the wall. God's enemy sang a terrifying song of defeat, a joyless cry of wailing pain from the captive of hell. Beowulf, the strongest man alive, held him fast.

Beowulf had no intention of letting the murderous monster escape alive, as his life was useless to anyone on earth. Many of Beowulf's warriors drew their ancestral swords, wanting to protect the life of their lord. They tried to strike from every side to kill Grendel, but they did not know the truth. No sword on earth, not even the finest iron weapon, could harm the sinful monster! The beast had cast a spell on all weapons and blades, making them useless against him.
