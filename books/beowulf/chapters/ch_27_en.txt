Chapter Twenty-Seven: The Underwater Battle and the Melted Blade

She quickly fought back, locking him in a tight, crushing grip. Weary in spirit, the strongest of warriors stumbled and fell to the ground. The monster sat upon him and drew her broad, bright-edged knife to avenge her only son. The strong chain mail on Beowulf's shoulder protected his life, stopping the blade from piercing his skin. The hero would have died deep beneath the earth if his armor had not helped him, and if Holy God, the Ruler of Heaven, had not guided the victory. Once Beowulf managed to stand up again, the Lord decided the matter justly.

Suddenly, he saw a legendary weapon hanging among the armor on the wall. It was an ancient sword made by giants in the old days, with exceptionally sharp edges. Although it was the finest of weapons, it was so massive that no other man could carry it into battle. Beowulf, fierce and grim, seized the decorated hilt. Despairing of his life but filled with anger, he drew the giant blade and struck a furious blow. The sword cut deep into her neck, shattering her neck bones and passing completely through her doomed body. She collapsed to the floor. The blade was stained with blood, and the hero rejoiced in his work.

A bright light shone inside the cave, as clear as the sun shining from the sky. Beowulf looked around the chamber and walked along the wall, holding the giant weapon firmly by its hilt. He was determined to repay Grendel for the many attacks he had launched against the Danes in the past. Grendel had slaughtered Hrothgar's companions in their sleep, devouring fifteen men and carrying off fifteen more as loot. The fierce champion avenged those crimes when he saw Grendel's corpse lying lifeless in the lair, weak from the mortal wound he had received at Heorot.

The corpse jerked when it suffered the blow after death. With one hard stroke of the giant sword, Beowulf cut off Grendel's head, finishing the task once and for all.
