Chapter Twenty-Eight: The Return to the Surface with Grendel's Head

The wise Danes who were watching the water with Hrothgar soon saw that the waves were disturbed and stained with blood. The gray-haired elders talked among themselves, fearing that the hero would not return victorious to their king. Many believed that the monster of the deep had destroyed him.

By the afternoon, the Danish warriors abandoned the cliff, and the king returned home in deep sorrow. The Geatish companions, however, stayed behind. They sat there, sick at heart, staring at the dark lake. They wished to see their beloved leader again, but they had no hope.

Meanwhile, the giant sword below the water began to melt away in bloody icicles. It was a great wonder that the blade melted just like ice when the Father loosens the bonds of frost and releases the cold water. He is the one who has power over times and seasons, the only true Creator.

The Geatish leader did not take any treasures from the lair, although he saw many there. He took only Grendel's head and the sword hilt, which was brightly decorated with gold. The steel blade had completely melted and burned away because the blood of the monster was so hot and poisonous.

Beowulf, having survived the onslaught of his enemies, began swimming up through the water. The surging waves and the vast depths were fully cleansed now that the monster had ended her life and left those waters.

The stout-hearted protector of the Seafarers swam to the shore, rejoicing in his heavy burden and the spoils of the sea. His loyal warriors ran to meet him, thanking God and rejoicing to see their leader safe and sound. They quickly loosened Beowulf's helmet and chain mail. The lake grew calm under the sky, the water still stained with blood.

They marched along the path, rejoicing and following the familiar road. Carrying Grendel's head from the cliff was a heavy task that required great effort. It took four warriors to carry the massive head on a spear shaft back to the royal hall. Soon, the fourteen Geats arrived at Heorot, and their proud leader walked with them across the plains.
