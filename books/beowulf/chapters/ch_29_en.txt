Chapter Twenty-Nine: The Presentation of the Trophies

The victorious leader, famous for his daring deeds and honored in battle, entered the hall to greet Hrothgar. Servants carried Grendel's head by its hair onto the floor of the feast-hall where the warriors were drinking. It was a terrifying sight for the men and the queen, and everyone stared at the wonderful trophy.

Beowulf, the son of Ecgtheow, spoke: "Behold, king Hrothgar! We have gladly brought you these spoils from the lake as a token of our victory. I barely survived that combat under the water. The struggle was difficult and stressful, and the fight would have ended in my death if God had not shielded me.

I could do nothing with Unferth's sword, Hrunting, even though it is an excellent blade. But the Ruler of Men guided me, showing me a massive, beautiful ancient sword hanging on the wall. He often helps those who have no other friends in their time of need. I drew the weapon and slew the monster in her home. When the hot blood burst out, the patterned blade melted away entirely. I carried the hilt back from the demons, and I have avenged the terrible deeds and deaths of the Danes, just as was fitting.

I promise you now that you may sleep in Heorot free from sorrow, along with all your warriors, both young and old. You no longer need to fear any threat of death to your people from that direction, noble king, as you did in the past."

Beowulf then presented the golden hilt, the ancient work of giants, to the old king Hrothgar. After the monsters fell, the hilt came into the possession of the Danish lord. When Grendel and his mother died, the hilt passed to the best king who ever ruled and distributed treasure in the northern lands.

Hrothgar spoke, examining the ancient family treasure. On it was carved the story of the ancient flood that destroyed the race of rebellious giants. Those giants were enemies of the Eternal Lord, so the Creator destroyed them with surging waters. On the golden cross-guard, rune letters clearly stated for whom this magnificent sword, with its twisted hilt and serpentine patterns, was first made.
