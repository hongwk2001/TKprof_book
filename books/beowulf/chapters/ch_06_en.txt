Chapter Six: The Meeting with King Hrothgar

Hrothgar, the king of the Danes, answered Wulfgar's message: "I knew Beowulf when he was just a young boy! His father was Ecgtheow, and his mother was the daughter of King Hrethel of the Geats. Now, Ecgtheow's brave son has come here, seeking a loyal friend. The sailors who brought our gifts to Geatland told me that this brave warrior has the strength of thirty men in his grip. I believe holy God has sent this hero to protect us Danes from the terror of Grendel. I will offer him rich treasures for his outstanding courage. Hurry! Tell the Geats to come in and meet our nobles. Let them know they are warmly welcomed by the Danish people."

Wulfgar went to the door of the hall and delivered the message to the guests: "My lord, the king of the Danes, knows of your noble family. He says that you are welcome here after your long journey across the sea. You may go inside wearing your armor and helmets to meet Hrothgar, but you must leave your shields and spears outside until your meeting is finished."

Beowulf stood up, surrounded by his impressive band of warriors. As he ordered, a few men stayed behind to guard their weapons. The rest hurried forward, led by Wulfgar into the great hall of Heorot. Beowulf marched proudly beneath his helmet until he stood directly before the king.
