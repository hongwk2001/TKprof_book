Chapter Eight: Hrothgar's Past and the Feast at Heorot

King Hrothgar replied: "You have come to us, my friend Beowulf, to fight in our defense and out of kindness. Your family has a long history with mine. Long ago, your father Ecgtheow started a massive feud when he killed Heatholaf of the Wylfing tribe. Fearing a bloody war, the Geats could not shelter your father, so he fled across the ocean waves to seek help from the Danes. At that time, I was a young king newly ruling the Danish people. My older brother, Heorogar, had just died, and he was a better man than I. To end your father's feud and save him, I sent rich treasures across the sea to the Wylfings. In return, your father swore an oath of loyalty to me.

It brings me great pain to tell anyone about the humiliation and ruin Grendel has caused us in Heorot. My warriors and guards are vanishing. Fate has swept them away into the monster's horror. Yet, God can easily stop this mad ravager! Many times, my warriors have gotten drunk on beer and sworn to wait in the feast-hall with their swords to fight Grendel. But when morning came, this beautiful hall was always stained with gore, and the benches were drenched in blood. Death has taken my trusted men away, leaving me with fewer veterans every day.

But sit down now to our feast! Relax and tell us stories of your victories, as your heart prompts you."

A bench was quickly cleared for the Geats in the feast-hall. The brave warriors sat down, proud of their strength. A servant walked around, carrying an ornate cup and pouring sweet mead. Occasionally, a poet sang with a clear voice in Heorot. There was great joy and laughter among the gathered Danes and Geats.
