Chapter Eleven: Queen Wealhtheow and the Night Watch

> *Night falls on Heorot, the warriors hold their breath for the coming blood-hunt.*

Laughter roared through Heorot. The mood was electric, heavy with the promise of blood and victory. Queen Wealhtheow stepped into the firelight, draped in hammered gold. She moved with a regal grace, navigating the sea of armed men.

First, she offered the jeweled drinking horn to Hrothgar. She wished the scarred king joy, a fierce reminder of the loyalty his people held for him. The old king drank deep, savoring the feast while he could.

The golden queen moved through the ranks, offering the cup to fresh-faced youths and battle-hardened veterans alike. Finally, her path led her to Beowulf.

She offered the goblet to the massive Geatish warlord. With sharp, knowing eyes, she thanked the gods that her desperate prayers were finally answered. Here was a true butcher of monsters—a champion she could trust to slaughter the nightmare plaguing her people.

Beowulf took the cup. His blood ran hot, hungry for the slaughter to come.

"When I launched my ships onto the black waves," Beowulf snarled, his voice booming over the quiet hall, "I swore a blood oath. I would rip this curse from your kingdom, or I would die choking in the monster's grip!"

He raised the cup, eyes blazing. "I will carve my legend in flesh and bone tonight, or this hall will become my tomb!"

The queen smiled. His violent vow was exactly what she needed to hear. Gleaming in gold, she returned to her king's side.

Courage flared through the hall once more. But the shadows were lengthening. Hrothgar knew the beast was coming. The second the sun vanished, the creature of the dark would stalk out from the night, hungry for slaughter.

The king stood, and the great hall fell silent. Hrothgar turned to Beowulf, locking eyes with the savage warrior.

"Never," Hrothgar boomed, "since I first learned to carry a shield and crack a skull, have I handed the keys of Heorot to another man. But tonight, it is yours."

The old king stepped closer. "Defend this glorious hall! Unleash your fury, watch the shadows, and remember the eternal glory of a warrior's death! Kill the beast and survive this night, and there is no treasure on earth I will deny you!"
