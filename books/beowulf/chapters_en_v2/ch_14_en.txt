Chapter Fourteen: The Victory and Grendel's Torn Arm

> *The feast-hall shatters, the tearing screams of the monster rip the darkness to shreds.*

Beowulf remembered his promise. He surged to his feet and clamped his hands around the monster. 

*Crack.* 

Grendel's fingers snapped under the crushing pressure. The giant thrashed, desperate to tear away, but the hero drove forward, tightening his iron grip. Panic seized the beast. He just wanted to run, to slither back to his swampy hell-hole, realizing his claws were caught in the most terrifying grasp on earth. Coming to Heorot was the worst mistake the destroyer had ever made.

The great hall exploded with the thunder of their brawl. Pure terror gripped the Danes as the two titans clashed, shaking the building to its core. 

It was a miracle the grand feast-hall didn't collapse under their absolute fury. It held fast, bound inside and out by masterful iron bands. Still, their brutal struggle ripped gold-plated benches straight from the floorboards. The Danes had sworn no living thing could ever break Heorot apart—only fire could consume it. Now, they weren't so sure.

A new, horrifying sound shattered the night. A shriek so foul it struck dread into every warrior who heard it echoing through the timber walls. 

It was the enemy of God, screaming a song of absolute defeat. A joyless, agonizing wail tearing from the throat of hell's own captive. 

Beowulf, the strongest man alive, refused to let go.

He had no intention of letting this murderous freak escape. Grendel's life was worth less than dirt. 

Around the brawl, Beowulf's warriors sprang into action. They drew their ancestral swords, rushing into the fray to protect their lord. They hacked and slashed from every angle, desperate to butcher the beast. 

But they didn't know the dark truth. No sword on earth, not even the finest forged steel, could scratch the monster. Grendel had woven a dark spell over all mortal blades, rendering them completely useless against his cursed hide.
