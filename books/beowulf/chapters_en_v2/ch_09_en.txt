Chapter Nine: Unferth's Challenge

> *The glory that silenced Unferth, laughter blooms once more in the golden feast-hall.*

Unferth, son of Ecglaf, sat at King Hrothgar’s feet. But he wasn’t there to celebrate. He was burning with jealousy over Beowulf’s rising legend. 

The Geat's sudden arrival—and the raw guts it took to cross the sea—rubbed the courtier the wrong way. Unferth couldn’t stomach the idea of another warrior snatching more glory than him. He wanted to tear Beowulf down.

"Are you that same Beowulf who raced Breca out on the open sea?" Unferth sneered, his voice cutting through the hall. 

"You two fools threw your lives away in the deep water! Just to prove a point! It was a stupid, empty boast, and no one could talk you out of that suicidal stunt. You thrashed through the ocean currents, fighting freezing, wintry waves while the sea boiled around you."

Unferth leaned forward. "You struggled in the icy depths for seven hellish nights. But guess what? Breca beat you. He overpowered you! When the sun finally rose, the current dumped him on the shores of the Heathoreams. He marched home a hero, ruling the Brondings and their golden city. Breca backed up his boast and left you in his wake."

He laughed coldly. "So I fully expect you to fail here, Beowulf. You might have survived a few petty skirmishes, but you won't last a single night waiting for Grendel in Heorot!"
