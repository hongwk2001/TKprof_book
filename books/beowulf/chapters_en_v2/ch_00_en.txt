Prologue: The First King, Scyld Scefing

> *Upon the blood and bones of great kings, the age of myth sets its glorious sail.*

Listen up! We all know the bloody legends of the ancient Danish kings. Back in the day, these warlords carved out their empires with steel and fury. 

Scyld Scefing was the most brutal of them all. He didn't just defeat his enemies—he smashed their mead-halls, shattered their armies, and struck pure terror into their hearts. 

He started from nothing. Found drifting on the brutal sea as a helpless, abandoned bastard. But he didn't stay weak for long. He grew into a titan under the heavens, crushing his trauma and forging his own honor in blood. 

Before long, every tribe across the freezing waves learned to fear him. They bent the knee, paid him tribute in heavy gold, and called him their master. Now *that* was a king.

God Himself took pity on the leaderless Danes and gave Scyld a son. The boy’s name was Beow. Heaven knew the people needed a champion, and Beow’s legendary name quickly echoed across the savage Northern lands. 

Even as a young prince, Beow knew the game. You bleed gold now so your warriors will bleed for you later. He showered his men with treasure. When war eventually crashed against their shores, his hardened fighters stood by his side, ready to butcher anyone who challenged him. Generosity buys loyalty, and loyalty wins wars.

When Scyld's time ran out, he died as he lived—a conqueror. His elite guard carried his body to the icy shoreline, executing the final orders of their warlord. 

A deadly, ice-clad warship waited in the harbor, its curved prow cutting the freezing wind. The ultimate hero’s vessel. 

His men laid their fallen king right at the base of the mast. They didn't bury him in dirt; they buried him in wealth. Piles of plundered treasure, gleaming armor, and razor-sharp weapons from across the world surrounded his lifeless body. 

No man had ever seen a more glorious funeral ship. It was a floating armory. A mountain of gold weighed down the king's chest, ready to sink with him into the abyss. They loaded him up with far more treasure than the pathetic scraps he had when he washed ashore as an orphan.

Finally, they hoisted a blazing golden banner high above his head, letting it snap in the howling wind. 

They shoved the ship out into the dark, violent ocean. The Danes stood on the freezing coast, their hearts heavy and their throats tight, watching their greatest king drift into the unknown. 

To this day, not the smartest scholars nor the most scarred veterans can tell you what shore Scyld Scefing finally washed up on.
