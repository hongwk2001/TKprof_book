Chapter Six: Face-to-Face with King Hrothgar

> *An oath to cast aside weapons, declaring a bare-handed bloodbath with the monster.*

Hrothgar, the scarred king of the Danes, took Wulfgar's report and cracked a grim smile. "I knew Beowulf when he was just a whelp! His bloodline is pure steel—son of Ecgtheow, born of Geatish royalty. Now, that boy has grown into a monster of a man, and he's come hunting for a fight alongside a true ally."

The king leaned forward, his eyes gleaming in the firelight. "My sailors claim this warrior packs the strength of thirty men in a single fist. I swear, the gods themselves must have hurled this champion at us to crush Grendel's skull! I'll shower him in gold if he has the guts to do it."

Hrothgar slammed his fist on the throne. "Move! Bring the Geats in. Let them stand among our fiercest lords and know the Danes embrace them!"

Wulfgar bolted to the hall's massive doors. He faced the waiting Geats and roared, "My king knows your bloodline! You've crossed the crushing sea, and he welcomes you to Heorot."

Wulfgar's gaze hardened. "You march in wearing your armor. Keep your helmets on. But leave your shields and killing steel out here until the king has taken your measure."

Beowulf rose like a titan of war, his elite squad falling into line behind him. With a sharp gesture, he ordered a handful of his men to hold the perimeter and guard their weapons.

The rest surged forward. Wulfgar led the heavy-armored strike team straight into the belly of Heorot. Beowulf didn't flinch. He marched like a storm tearing through the great hall, his helmet gleaming with raw menace, until he planted his boots dead center before the Danish king.
