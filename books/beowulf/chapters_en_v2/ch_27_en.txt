Chapter 27: The Underwater Battle and the Melted Blade

> *A horrific underwater bloodbath with the Hag, even the trusted legendary sword betrays him against her tough hide.*

The she-wolf struck back with vicious speed, clamping Beowulf in a bone-crushing grip. Exhausted and battered, the strongest warrior on earth staggered—and fell.

The monster straddled him instantly. She whipped out a broad, razor-sharp hunting knife, her eyes burning with vengeance for her butchered son. She drove the blade down hard. 

But Beowulf's chain mail held. The forged rings bit into his shoulder, deflecting the lethal strike. If that armor had snapped, the great hero would have bled out deep beneath the earth. But God and good steel watched over him. With a roar of defiance, Beowulf surged to his feet, turning the tide of the fight.

Then, he saw it. 

Hanging among the rusted trophies on the cave wall was a weapon of myth. A massive, ancient sword forged by giants in the world's dawn. Its edge gleamed with impossible sharpness. It was the absolute pinnacle of weaponry, so heavy that no ordinary man could even lift it. 

Beowulf wasn't ordinary. 

Grim and furious, he wrapped his hands around the ornate hilt. He ripped the colossal blade from the wall. Channeling every ounce of his wrath, he swung the giant sword in a devastating arc. 

The edge sheared straight through her neck. Bone shattered. Muscle tore. The blade cleaved her doomed body completely in two. She crashed to the cavern floor in a heap of gore. 

The giant sword dripped with steaming blood. Beowulf smiled. The work was good.

Suddenly, the grim cavern flared with brilliant light, as blazing and pure as the midday sun. Gripping the giant hilt, Beowulf stalked along the cave wall, hunting for a final piece of payback. 

He hadn't forgotten the slaughter. He remembered Grendel ripping Hrothgar's sleeping men to shreds, swallowing fifteen warriors whole and dragging fifteen more back to this hellhole as meat. 

The fierce champion found what he was looking for. Grendel's massive corpse lay slumped in a corner, drained of life from the arm-rending wound he took at Heorot.

Even in death, the brute needed a lesson. 

Beowulf brought the giant sword down in one colossal stroke. The headless corpse violently twitched as the blade sheared through its spine. Grendel's hideous head hit the floor.

The job was finally done.
