Chapter Thirty-Six: The Fifty-Year Reign and the Dragon's Fire

> *Fifty years of peace engulfed in flames, the greed of a thief touches the reverse scale of a sleeping dragon.*

A dragon claimed the dark nights. He guarded a massive treasure hoard buried in a steep stone mound high on a heath. A secret path twisted beneath the earth, unknown to men—until a thief stumbled inside. 

He found the ancient hoard and stole a massive, jeweled gold cup. 

When the dragon awoke and saw his prize missing, a roaring fury shook the earth. Someone was going to pay. The surrounding lands would soon burn with the beast’s terrible rage.

The thief hadn’t planned to rob a monster. He was a desperate slave, running from his master's brutal beatings, seeking any shelter he could find. When he crawled into the dark mound, terror gripped him. But greed overpowered his panic. The miserable slave snatched the precious cup and sprinted back into the night.

The stone chamber held a mountain of ancient treasures. In the days of old, a lone survivor of a forgotten, noble bloodline had buried his people’s vast legacy there. Death had slaughtered his kin, leaving him the last warrior standing. Knowing his days were numbered, he built a reinforced fortress near the crashing waves, locked away by treacherous paths. 

The lonely keeper hauled the massive wealth of gold and jewels into the dark, roaring these final words:

"Hold this wealth, earth! Since heroes cannot, you take it back! Brave men bled to rip it from you, and now battle-death has butchered every last warrior of my tribe. Their feast-hall joys are dead. I have no one to swing the sword or polish the gold cup. My brothers are gone!

"The gold-plated helmet will rot. The men who shined our war-masks are sleeping in the dirt. The heavy armor that stopped iron blades and shattered shields decays with the warrior. The chain mail dies with the king! There’s no harp, no song, no hawk diving through the hall, and no warhorse thundering in the courtyard. Death has wiped my people from the earth!"

Broken and bitter, he mourned alone. He wept day and night until death finally swallowed him.

Then the dragon came. The beast of twilight found the open hoard. This fiery nightmare flew through the night, hunting for burial mounds, striking terror into the hearts of men. His nature drove him to hoard earthly gold, guarding wealth he could never spend.

For three hundred years, the monstrous terror guarded that underground vault. Then the slave sparked his wrath. 

The desperate thief brought the gold cup to his master, begging for his life. His lord took the ancient treasure, and the slave bought his pardon.

But in the mound, the dragon woke up. 

His fury ignited. He sniffed the stone, tracking the thief’s footprints. The man had sneaked right past the sleeping beast's head! Only a man protected by God could survive such a reckless gamble. 

The dragon scoured the ground, frantic to find the rat who robbed him. Hot and bloodthirsty, he circled the mound, but the wasteland was empty. He plunged back inside, confirming the agonizing truth: the golden cup was gone.

The monster paced, starving for nightfall. Bursting with infernal rage, the beast swore to avenge his stolen cup in a storm of fire. 

The moment the sun died, the dragon launched into the sky, vomiting flames. It was a terrifying nightmare for the people of the land—and a disaster that would soon bring their king straight into the fire.
