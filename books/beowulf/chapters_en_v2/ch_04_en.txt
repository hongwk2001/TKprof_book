Chapter Four: The Arrival

> *The unstoppable march of a hero, knocking on the heavy doors of the great golden hall.*

The leader of the band stepped up, his voice slicing through the wind. 

"We are Geats," he declared. "Sworn blades of King Hygelac. My father was the warlord Ecgtheow. He carved a bloody legend, lived a long life, and died a respected man. Wise men everywhere still speak his name with reverence."

He met the guard's gaze without blinking. "We come in peace, seeking your lord, Hrothgar, King of the Danes. Show us the way. We carry a critical message for your king, and we don't hide in the shadows."

He leaned forward, his voice dropping to a dangerous rumble. "You know if the rumors are true. We heard some nightmare is butchering your men—a phantom spreading slaughter and shame through the black nights. I've come to give Hrothgar the strategy he needs to crush this demon. I will give him his peace, or he will suffer until his beautiful hall is nothing but ash."

The coast-guard tightened his grip on his spear and spoke from his warhorse. 

"A real warrior knows the difference between empty talk and cold steel. But I believe you. I see you are true allies to the Danish king."

He gestured inland. "March forward with your weapons bare. I will lead you to his court. My men will guard your ship on the sand, keeping it safe until you sail back to Geatland. A warrior who marches into danger with that kind of fire is bound to survive the slaughter."

They moved out, leaving their ship anchored tight against the surf. 

The Geat warriors marched in tight formation. Golden boars snarled from the crests of their heavy helmets, ancient wards forged to cheat death in battle. They pushed a relentless pace until Hrothgar's legendary hall broke the horizon. 

It was a fortress wrapped in gold. The most glorious building in the known world, its light burning like a beacon across the brutal lands. 

The guard pointed his spear at the shining mead-hall. "There it is. March straight for the doors." 

He wheeled his heavy horse around. "My watch calls me back to the shore. May the Almighty shield you in the blood to come. I ride to hunt for raiders."
