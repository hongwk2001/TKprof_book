Chapter Five: The King's Herald, Wulfgar

> *The arrival of the burdened one, a savior stands before the eyes of Hrothgar.*

A cobblestone road led the Geatish warband straight to the towering mead-hall of Heorot. Iron rings clattered and chainmail scraped as they marched, battle-hardened and utterly fearless. 

Weary from the violent ocean crossing, they unslung their heavy shields and slammed them against Heorot’s timber walls. The men threw themselves onto the wooden benches, their armor chiming like a death knell. They stacked their deadly ash-wood spears in the corner—gray iron tips gleaming with brutal promise. These weren't mere travelers; they were a strike force bristling for war.

A proud Danish officer strode out to block their path. He eyed their gear and demanded their names. 

"Where did you haul these gilded shields, these scarred helmets, and those lethal spears?" he barked. "I am Hrothgar’s herald. And I’ll tell you this: I’ve never seen a band of strangers walk onto our shores looking this dangerous. You didn't come here running as exiles. You came here hunting for glory."

Beowulf, the giant of a man leading the Geats, didn't flinch. From beneath the shadow of his war-helm, his voice rumbled with absolute calm. 

"We are King Hygelac’s chosen," Beowulf said. "And my name is Beowulf. I have a mission for Hrothgar, your legendary king. Take me to him."

The herald was Wulfgar, a chieftain of the Wendels known just as much for his cunning mind as his bloody blade. He nodded, recognizing raw power when he saw it. 

"I will take your words to the Lord of the Danes," Wulfgar promised. "I'll deliver your demand and bring back his command—whatever it may be."

Wulfgar spun on his heel and marched into the great hall. He wove through the crowded benches until he stood before Hrothgar, the old and battle-scarred king, flanked by his fiercest warlords. 

Wulfgar offered a crisp, formal salute, then broke the silence. 

"My King, a warband from Geatland has just made landfall. They tore through the ocean to get here. Their commander is a beast of a man named Beowulf." 

Wulfgar lowered his voice, his tone urgent. "They want an audience with you, my lord. Do not turn them away. I’ve seen their armor, and I’ve seen their eyes. They are elite killers, and the warlord who leads them is a force of nature."
