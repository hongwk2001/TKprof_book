Chapter Three: The Hero of Geatland, Beowulf

> *Giants piercing through the storm, the Geat warriors drop anchor on the shores of the Danes.*

Hrothgar sat in his misery. The grief was endless, gnawing at the old king. No wisdom or wealth could cure the bleeding wound Grendel had torn in his kingdom. The butchery was relentless, disgusting, and completely merciless. It was Denmark's worst nightmare, dragged out year after bloody year.

Across the bitter sea in Geatland, the stories of Grendel's slaughter reached a warrior named Beowulf. He served King Hygelac. Beowulf was no ordinary man. He was the most massive, dangerous, and powerful fighter alive.

He didn't hesitate. Beowulf ordered his men to prep a heavy warship. 

"The Danish king needs killers to defend his hall," Beowulf declared. "I'm crossing the ocean to answer."

His people loved him, and the elders dreaded seeing him leave, but they wouldn't dare hold him back. They read the omens, cheered the hero on, and prepared his men for war.

Beowulf handpicked fourteen of the most lethal Geatish warriors. They marched to the shore, guided by a hardened sea captain. The sea churned against the sand below the high cliffs. They piled onto the timber ship, loading the hull with gleaming chainmail, heavy shields, and deadly weapons. With a massive heave, they thrust the warship into the open sea.

The wind grabbed the sails. The ship tore across the waves like a bird of prey, cutting through the whitecaps. By the second day, the opposite shoreline erupted from the horizon. 

They locked eyes on steep hills, shining sea-cliffs, and broad headlands. The crossing was done. The Geats hit the beach hard, tying the longship off and jumping into the surf. Their heavy armor rattled with every step. They offered a swift prayer to God for a fast, unbroken crossing.

High on the bluffs, the Danish coast guard spotted them. He watched heavily armed strangers hauling shields and spears down the gangplank. 

The guard mounted his warhorse, charged down to the shoreline, and shook a massive spear in their faces.

"Who the hell are you?" the guard roared. "You sail a warship onto our shores, fully armed and locked in chainmail. I watch these cliffs to keep raiders out of Denmark, and nobody just lands here in broad daylight. You have no passwords. You have no permission."

He locked eyes with Beowulf and paused. 

"I've never seen a warrior as massive as your leader," he admitted, lowering his spear an inch. "You're no standard guard. You carry yourself like a warlord. But before you take one more step onto Danish soil, I need to know your bloodlines. No spies walk out of here alive. Speak up, and make it quick."
