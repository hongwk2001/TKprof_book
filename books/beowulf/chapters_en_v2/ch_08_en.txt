Chapter Eight: Blood Debts and Bitter Mead

> *Blood in the crashing waves, recounting the legendary tearing of nine sea beasts.*

King Hrothgar locked eyes with the Geatish champion. 

"You didn't just come for glory, Beowulf," Hrothgar rumbled. "You came to settle an old blood debt. Your father, Ecgtheow, sparked a massive war when he slaughtered Heatholaf of the Wylfing tribe. The Geats were terrified of the fallout. They exiled him, and he fled across the crashing waves to my shores."

Hrothgar sighed, the weight of years pressing into his shoulders. "I was young then. A newly crowned king following the death of my brother, Heorogar—a man far better than I'll ever be. I bought your father's life. I shipped chests of gold across the sea to the Wylfings to extinguish the feud, and Ecgtheow swore his loyalty to me until his dying breath."

The king’s voice tightened with simmering rage. "It burns my soul to admit what Grendel has done to Heorot. My best men are vanishing into the dark. Fate feeds them straight into the jaws of that monster! I know the Almighty could crush this demon in an instant, but until then, we bleed." 

Hrothgar slammed a fist onto the armrest. "Countless times, my warriors have pounded back ale and sworn on their swords to ambush Grendel in the night. And every morning? The sun rises on a slaughterhouse. Benches splintered. The floor slick with guts and gore. I lose more veterans with every sunrise."

The old king forced a weary smile and waved his hand. "Enough of this darkness. Sit down! Drink! Feast! Regale us with the bloody tales of your victories!" 

Instantly, a massive wooden bench was cleared for the Geats. Beowulf and his heavily armed crew took their seats, radiating quiet menace and raw power. Servants scrambled to fill their heavy goblets with sweet, potent mead. 

A bard struck up a fierce, ringing song, cutting through the shadows in Heorot. For the first time in years, the great hall echoed with the booming laughter of Danes and Geats, united in the face of death.
