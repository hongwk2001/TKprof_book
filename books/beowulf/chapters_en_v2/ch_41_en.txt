Chapter Forty-One: The Death of the Dragon

> *The horrific end of the dragon, torn by the monster's fangs, the old king uses his last strength to sever the disaster's throat.*

The fire-dragon surged for a third strike. The Geats’ nightmare lunged at Beowulf, seizing the moment. Its jaws snapped shut, sinking razor-sharp fangs deep into the hero’s neck. Boiling life-blood sprayed, painting the king’s armor crimson.

But Wiglaf refused to watch his king die. Ignoring the inferno, he unleashed the raw fury in his blood. He didn’t waste time on the beast’s armored skull. Instead, he drove his golden blade straight up into the dragon's soft belly. Flames licked his hand, searing his flesh, but he pushed the steel deeper.

Instantly, the monster's fire sputtered.

The pain jolted Beowulf back to his senses. His iron will flared. He ripped the battle-knife from his mail-shirt and swung with everything he had left. The brutal slash cleaved the serpent clean in half. 

Together, the two blood-relatives had crushed the terror of the skies. That is what a true warrior does.

But victory came at a lethal price. The dragon’s venom boiled inside Beowulf’s chest, burning him from the inside out. Staggering, fighting to keep his mind clear, the king slumped against the cavern wall. He stared up at the massive stone arches, marveling at the towering architecture of ancient giants.

Wiglaf moved fast. He washed the bloody gashes and tore off the king's helmet, fighting to buy Beowulf a few more breaths.

Beowulf forced words through the agony. He knew the end had come. His time on earth was bleeding out.

"I’d hand this armor to my son—if God had given me an heir," Beowulf rasped, his voice echoing in the stone vault. "Fifty years I've held this throne. Fifty years, and no king on earth dared cross my borders or test my steel. I fought only the battles I had to. I kept my word, and I never spilled the blood of my own kin."

He managed a grim, blood-stained smile. "When my soul leaves this ruined shell, the Creator can't condemn me. I die with honor."

His eyes locked onto Wiglaf. "Now go. Sprint down into the vault and drag out the hoard. The beast is dead, and its gold is ours. I want to see the ancient treasure, the gold and the flashing gems. Let me look upon the wealth I won... it will make it easier to close my eyes, and leave the kingdom I protected for so long."
