Chapter Thirty-Three: The Return to Geatland and the Tale of Modthryth

> *The Geat ship cuts through the sea, hoisting sails for their glorious homeland with overflowing golden spoils.*

Beowulf marched from Heorot, a conquering hero draped in gold. His blood hummed with victory. Down at the roaring surf, his broad ship tugged at its anchor, hungry for the open sea.

As the Geats swaggered toward the coast, they roared praises for Hrothgar’s endless generosity. He was a flawless king—until old age, the one enemy no blade can cut, finally bled away his strength. 

The heavy tramp of iron boots and the rattle of mail-shirts announced the young warriors' arrival at the shore. The Danish coast guard spotted them instantly. This time, there was no challenge from the cliffs, no spears leveled in suspicion. Instead, he spurred his horse down the slopes to greet them like brothers. 

"Your people are going to throw one hell of a feast when they see you bright-armored bastards marching home!" he shouted.

They loaded the curved-prow ship with a king's ransom. Armor, warhorses, and Hrothgar's staggering hoards of treasure were packed tight. The mast scraped the sky over a fortune in gold. Before leaving, Beowulf tossed the boat guard a magnificent gold-bound sword. The guard’s eyes went wide—that blade would make him a legend on the mead-hall benches. 

Beowulf vaulted aboard. They cast off, plunging into the deep currents, leaving the Danish shores behind.

They hoisted the sail, and the canvas snapped with a crack like thunder. The heavy timber groaned, but the gale pushed them faster. The ship carved through the foaming waves, tearing a path across the black ocean. 

Soon, the towering cliffs and jagged headlands of Geatland broke the horizon. Driven by the fierce wind, the keel slammed hard into the sandy beach. They were home.

The harbor guard was already sprinting down the shore. He’d been staring at the skyline for weeks, praying for their return. He slammed heavy anchor ropes into the sand, making damn sure the surf didn't drag the longship back out. 

Beowulf didn’t waste a second. "Unload the gold!" he commanded. 

They hauled the massive chests of treasure onto the beach. They didn't have far to walk. King Hygelac’s mighty fortress stood just up the ridge, a towering stronghold looming right over the sea wall. 

Inside, the hall was a marvel. King Hygelac reigned with absolute power, and at his side sat Queen Hygd. Young, fiercely smart, and undeniably noble, she had only spent a few winters in the castle. But her hands were always open. She rained wealth and gold down on the Geatish warriors, earning their absolute loyalty.

She was nothing like the legendary Queen Modthryth. 

In the old days, Modthryth was a nightmare—a beautiful tyrant drunk on arrogance and blood. Any warrior stupid enough to look her directly in the eyes found out exactly how cruel she could be. Her guards would tackle him, bind him in heavy ropes, and drag him away. A decorated sword would flash, and his head would hit the floor. 

It was a hell of a way to run a kingdom. A queen should be handing out rings, not severing the necks of loyal men over a stray glance. 

But her bloodthirsty reign didn't last. The grizzled warriors loved to tell the tale of how she was tamed over their ale-horns. At her father’s command, she was shipped across the sea to marry Offa, the young champion. 

Marriage to Offa broke her cruel streak. Sitting on his throne, she swapped the executioner's axe for genuine grace. She became a queen renowned for her goodness and her fierce devotion to her king. And Offa? He was a warlord of unmatched courage, a generous ruler feared and respected for his raw wisdom and crushing victories in battle.
