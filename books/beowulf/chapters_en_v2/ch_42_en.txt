Chapter Forty-Two: The Treasure Hoard Revealed

> *The cursed treasure vault revealed to the world, the fatally wounded king confirms the gold for his people.*

Wiglaf didn't hesitate. Obeying his bleeding, battle-torn king, the young warrior plunged into the dragon’s stone barrow. 

Armor clinking in the dark, he pushed past massive stone pillars and stopped dead. Victory surged through him. The beast's lair was drowning in riches. 

Glittering gold carpeted the rocky earth. Breathtaking tapestries clung to the cavern walls. He found ancient drinking horns from forgotten kings, rusted battle-helms, and thick armbands woven from solid gold. 

Buried wealth carries a curse. It twists the souls of the living, tearing down empires no matter how deep you bury it. 

High above the mounds of loot hung a heavy golden banner, a masterpiece of war-weaving. It practically glowed in the dark, casting a fierce light across the cavern floor. By its glare, Wiglaf swept his eyes over the ancient hoard. 

The dragon was nowhere to be seen. Beowulf’s iron had gutted the beast for good.

Wiglaf tore into the hoard. He grabbed handfuls of ancient gold, burying his arms in jeweled cups and heavy plates. He ripped the glowing golden banner down from its perch. 

The midnight terror that had guarded this wealth for centuries was finally dead—slaughtered by a true king's blade.

Loaded down with the spoils of war, Wiglaf sprinted back toward the surface. Panic clawed at his throat. Would Beowulf still draw breath?

He found his lord slumped on the cliffside, soaked in his own blood and fading fast. Wiglaf dropped the treasure and scrambled to his king, desperately splashing cold water onto his ruined face. 

Slowly, the old warrior stirred. Fighting through the haze of death, Beowulf gathered his fading strength to speak one last time.
