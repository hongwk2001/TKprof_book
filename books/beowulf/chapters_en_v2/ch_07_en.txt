Chapter Seven: Beowulf's Pledge to Fight Grendel

> *Provoked by the jealousy of Unferth, the hero answers with a tale of sea-monster slaughter.*

Beowulf spoke, his iron mail clinking and gleaming like silver fire in the torchlight. 

"Hail, Hrothgar!" he roared. "I am King Hygelac's nephew, his fiercest warrior. I've bathed in the blood of my enemies since I was a boy. Word of Grendel's butchery reached my homeland. Sailors told us how this mighty hall sits empty, a ghost town the moment the sun dips below the horizon.

"My elders urged me to come. They know my power. They've seen me return from slaughter, soaked in the gore of my foes. I chained five giants, wiped their clan from the earth, and strangled sea-beasts in the freezing ocean depths. I crushed those who dared harm my people. Now, Grendel is mine. I will face this demon alone.

"So I ask one favor, Lord of the Danes. Do not deny me, for I have crossed the world for this fight. Let me and my men alone cleanse Heorot. 

"They say this abomination scorns weapons in his bloodlust. So be it. To honor my uncle, King Hygelac, I won't touch a sword or shield. I will meet the fiend with my bare hands. Flesh against flesh, life against life. Whoever dies will fall to the judgment of God!

"If the beast wins, he will gorge on my warriors right here, just as he slaughtered yours. You won't need to dig a grave. He will drag my shredded corpse back to his swamp and feast on my bones. Don't waste time mourning. 

"But if I fall, send my armor back to Hygelac. It's an ancient treasure forged by Weland himself. Let fate decide our bloody end!"
