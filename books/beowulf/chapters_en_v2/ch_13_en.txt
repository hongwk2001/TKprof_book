Chapter Thirteen: The Battle in the Hall

> *A massacre unfolding in the blink of an eye, and finally, the explosive grip of the hero.*

Grendel stalked from the misty moors, carrying the wrath of God himself. The twisted beast hungered for human flesh, his sights set on the great hall. He slinked through the fog until he spotted Heorot—the gold-trimmed fortress of men, gleaming like a beacon in the night. 

He'd raided Hrothgar's home plenty of times before. But tonight, his luck had run out. The monster was about to meet the deadliest guard he would ever face.

The hell-spawn reached the heavy doors. Forged iron bands held them shut, but they shattered like glass the second he shoved them. Fueled by demonic rage, Grendel ripped the entrance wide open and stomped onto the polished floor. 

A sickly, unnatural fire burned in his eyes. He scanned the dark room and saw a goldmine of sleeping heroes—warriors packed shoulder to shoulder, dead to the world. Grendel's twisted heart pounded with glee. He planned to slaughter every last one of them before sunrise. It was going to be a bloodbath. A feast fit for a demon.

But fate had a different plan. After tonight, Grendel would never taste human flesh again. 

From the shadows, Beowulf lay awake. Hygelac's champion watched the beast with predatory focus, waiting to see his opening move. Grendel wasted no time. He lunged at a sleeping warrior, tearing the man to shreds in seconds. He crunched through bone, drank hot blood straight from the veins, and devoured him in massive, ravenous bites. Hands, feet, everything—swallowed whole. Nothing was left.

Still hungry, the demon crept closer. He reached out a massive, clawed hand to rip Beowulf from his bed. 

But as the monster lunged, Beowulf struck. 

The hero clamped his iron-hard fingers around Grendel's outstretched arm, pivoting his weight and locking the beast in a crushing death-grip. The moment those fingers dug into his flesh, panic spiked through the monster's veins. In all his miserable life, Grendel had never felt strength like this. No mortal man possessed power so terrifying.

Frantic, the beast tried to jerk away, but he was trapped in a vise. Pure fear seized his mind. He just wanted to break free, run back to the dark, and hide with the rest of hell's rejects. But there was no escape. Beowulf had him, and Heorot was about to become the monster's slaughterhouse.
