Chapter One: Heorot and the Beast

> *A curse strikes the golden hall, and the songs of celebration drown in bloody screams.*

Beow the Dane held the throne for years, a beloved king stepping into the massive shoes of his father, Scyld. Beow's bloodline ran hot and heavy, passing on to his son, Half-Dane—a ruthless warlord who ruled with an iron fist and a sharp mind.

Half-Dane crushed all opposition, leaving behind a prosperous kingdom and four heirs: Heorogar, Hrothgar, Halga the Good, and a fierce daughter who took the hand of the Swedish King, Onela.

It was Hrothgar who truly forged a legacy of blood and glory. Success followed him like a shadow, and his warband swelled into an unstoppable army. Flush with victory, Hrothgar commanded the construction of a monument to his dominance: a feast-hall to dwarf all others.

He ordered tribes from every corner of the earth to build and decorate a hall unlike anything the world had ever seen. When the dust settled, the massive stronghold stood complete. He named it Heorot.

Inside those towering walls, Hrothgar made good on his promises. He showered his warriors with gold, rings, and looted treasures. Heorot was a towering symbol of power, a glittering beacon in a dark world—even though it was doomed to burn in the ashes of a future blood feud.

But the booming laughter echoing from Heorot woke something terrible.

Deep in the rotting swamps, a creature of pure malice writhed in agony. Every day, the relentless sounds of celebration tore at his sanity. The plucking of harps. The thundering stomps of drunken warriors. The poets singing of the Almighty ripping the earth from the waters and hanging the sun in the sky. 

The noise drove him mad.

His name was Grendel.

He was a nightmare forged in hellfire, a hulking abomination stalking the shadowed borders of the kingdom. Cursed by God as a blood-relative of Cain, Grendel was born from the first murder. From that ancient sin spawned every giant, ghoul, and demon that crawled the earth—and Grendel was the deadliest of them all.

Now, he set his sights on Heorot. The age of happiness for Hrothgar's men was over. The slaughter was about to begin.
