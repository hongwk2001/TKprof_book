Chapter Two: Grendel's Bloodlust and the Fall of Heorot

> *The Danes fall into despair, shedding tears of blood as they wait for salvation.*

Night fell, and Grendel stalked toward the great hall. The demon wanted to see how the legendary Danish warriors spent their time after draining their mead horns. He found them sprawled across the floor, dead to the world in a drunken sleep, completely oblivious to the nightmare closing in.

The starved beast didn't hesitate. He struck with savage fury.

Clawing through the darkness, Grendel ripped thirty warriors straight from their beds. He didn't just kill them—he butchered them. Hoisting their mangled bodies over his massive shoulders, he tore back to his swampy lair, roaring in triumph over his bloody prize. 

When dawn broke, the surviving warriors woke to a slaughterhouse. The joyous echoes of last night's feast were dead, replaced by the screams of the living. 

King Hrothgar, the great and famous warlord, slumped in his high seat, utterly broken. His heart shattered as he stared at the bloody footprints left by the cursed fiend. This wasn't a battle—it was a slaughter. The horror was too raw, the loss too massive.

And it wasn't over. 

Just one night later, Grendel returned for more blood. He felt no pity, no remorse. His soul was rotting with pure, unadulterated evil. 

After that second strike, it didn't take a genius to realize Heorot was a death trap. Men scattered, choosing to sleep far away from the grand hall. Once the monster’s relentless bloodlust became undeniable, anyone with half a brain abandoned the golden mead-hall. Fleeing was the only way to stay alive.

Grendel took the throne of darkness. He waged a one-monster war against the Danes, fighting dirty and tearing them apart until the once-glorious Heorot stood totally abandoned. 

For twelve agonizing years, King Hrothgar choked on his grief. The brutal truth spread like wildfire across the lands. Bards sang grim tales of Grendel’s endless war against Hrothgar—a relentless, bloody campaign season after season. The demon didn't want a truce. He didn't care about gold, treaties, or paying the blood-price. Nobody bargained with this beast.

He was the shadow of death, hunting seasoned veterans and young boys alike. Night after night, Grendel ruled the misty moors. No mortal could track where these hell-spawn disappeared to when the sun came up.

The enemy of mankind made Heorot his personal playground in the dark. He committed unspeakable horrors within those beautiful walls. But there was one thing he could never do: he could never touch Hrothgar's sacred throne. The beast was entirely cursed, barred forever from the warmth of God's love and the holy seat of power.

It was a nightmare era for Hrothgar. His surviving warlords scrambled, holding desperate councils to figure out a battle plan against the unstoppable force. When steel and strategy failed, they turned to blind desperation. They burned offerings at pagan shrines, screaming into the void for their old gods to save them. 

It was all they knew. They cast their thoughts down to the fires of hell, blind to the true Creator, the Judge of all deeds. They didn't know how to call upon the Lord of Heaven, and so the darkness raged on.
