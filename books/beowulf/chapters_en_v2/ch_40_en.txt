Chapter Forty: Wiglaf's Stand

> *Those who fled and the one who remained, the loyal comrade Wiglaf charges into the flames to protect his liege.*

His name was Wiglaf. Son of Weohstan. A shield-smashing noble of Swedish blood, and kin to Aelfhere. He watched his king roasting alive beneath his iron mask, and the sight burned into his soul. Wiglaf remembered the spoils Beowulf had showered upon him: the rich lands of the Waegmundings and the birthrights of his father. He couldn't just stand there. His hand clamped around his golden-wood shield. He drew his ancient sword.

That blade was a family legend. It once belonged to Eanmund, son of Ohthere. Weohstan had slaughtered Eanmund in single combat when the prince was a friendless exile, stripping the corpse of its dark helm, ringed chainmail, and ancient steel. King Onela had actually rewarded Weohstan with that very armor—even though Eanmund was Onela’s own nephew. Blood didn't matter; victory did. Weohstan hoarded the gear for decades until Wiglaf came of age to carve his own bloody path. When Weohstan finally died, he passed down a mountain of war-gear to his son.

Now, it was the young warrior's turn to bleed beside his lord. His courage didn't melt, and his father's sword wouldn't fail. The dragon was about to find out exactly what Wiglaf was made of.

Wiglaf spun on his cowardly companions, his voice dripping with venom. "I remember the mead-hall! We drank his honey-wine and swore on our blades that we'd repay him for the armor and weapons on our backs! Our king picked us from the entire army! He chose us for glory! He handed us treasure because he believed we were ruthless warriors. He wanted to butcher this monster alone because he is the greatest champion alive.

"But now? Our king needs us! Draw your steel! Move in and fight while the inferno rages! By the gods, I'd rather the fire burn my flesh to ash alongside my king than cower in the dirt! It's a coward's disgrace to haul our shields back home without killing the beast and saving our lord. Beowulf didn't bleed for us just to die alone! We share the sword, the helmet, and the mail-shirt! We fight together!"

Wiglaf didn't wait for them. He plunged into the choking, venomous smoke, raising his helm to cover his king. He shouted over the roaring flames: "Beloved Beowulf! Fight! Keep the oath you swore in your youth! You promised your glory would never die. Now, champion, fight for your life with everything you have! I am with you!"

The dragon roared and charged a second time. A tidal wave of hellfire slammed into them. Wiglaf’s wooden shield instantly vaporized into ash. His mail-shirt was useless against the heat. But the young warrior didn't flinch. He dove behind Beowulf’s massive iron shield just as his own disintegrated.

The heat re-ignited the war king's legendary fury. Beowulf swung his sword with apocalyptic force, burying the blade deep into the dragon's skull. 

CRACK. 

Naegling snapped! Beowulf's trusted blade shattered into useless shrapnel. Iron was never his destiny. His monstrous strength was a curse—his grip was too devastating, and he broke every sword he swung. Even the strongest steel was a liability in the hands of Beowulf.
