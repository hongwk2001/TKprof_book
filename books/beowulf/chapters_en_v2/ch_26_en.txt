Chapter Twenty-Six: The Giant Sword and the Swim to the Cave

> *The entrance to the terrifying swamp, the hero vows bloody revenge and dives into the crimson water.*

Beowulf, son of Ecgtheow, turned to the king. "Remember our pact, Hrothgar! I plunge into hell now. If I don't crawl back, protect my men. Treat my young wolves as your own."

He pointed to his hoard. "Send that gold back to Hygelac. Let my king see the spoils I bled for. Let him know I served a generous lord. As for Unferth—let that loudmouth have my old family sword. I will carve out glory with Hrunting, or death can take me!"

He didn't wait for an answer. The Geatish warlord dove straight into the abyss.

The black, boiling lake swallowed him whole. Down he plunged through the crushing dark. It felt like an eternity before his boots hit the muddy bottom.

The swamp hag had haunted this watery grave for fifty years. She sensed the intruder immediately. Lunging upward, she snatched the hero in her foul, crushing grip. But she couldn't tear his flesh. His battle-hardened chain mail held firm, grinding against her filthy claws.

The sea-wolf dragged Beowulf deeper into her submerged lair. She pinned his arms so tight he couldn't swing his blade. Blood-crazed water-beasts swarmed them in the depths, smashing their tusks against his armor in a tearing frenzy.

Suddenly, the crushing water broke. Beowulf found himself slammed onto dry stone. He was inside a massive, domed cavern, sealed off from the lake's killing currents. A roaring fire burned in the center, illuminating the nightmare.

There she stood—the monstrous swamp hag.

Beowulf roared and swung Hrunting with all his brutal strength. The heavy steel cracked down on her skull like a thunderbolt.

*CLANG!*

The legendary blade bounced right off. The edge completely failed. A sword that had sheared through a thousand helmets and spilled an ocean of blood had just met its match. For the first time in its bloody history, it didn't leave a scratch.

Beowulf didn't panic. He just got angrier. 

The warlord hurled the useless, jeweled sword against the cavern wall. Steel wouldn't end this. He'd rip her apart with his bare hands. 

This is the way of a true warrior. If you want immortal glory, you fight like you're already dead.

Boiling with rage, Beowulf launched himself at Grendel's mother. He grabbed the screaming hag by the shoulder and hurled her violently to the stone floor. It was time to end it.
