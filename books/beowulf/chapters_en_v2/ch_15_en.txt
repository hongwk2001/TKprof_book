Chapter Fifteen: The Defeat of Grendel

> *The demon flees with a fatal wound, a perfect morning finally dawns on the cursed hall.*

Grendel was doomed. Destined for a miserable, agonizing death, his evil spirit was holding a one-way ticket to hell. The monster had waged a brutal war against mankind and God himself, but now, he realized the terrifying truth: his own body was failing him. 

Beowulf crushed the beast's hand in an iron grip. The hatred radiating between them was absolute and deadly. 

Then came the horrific, fatal injury. A massive, gaping tear ripped open Grendel's shoulder. Sinews snapped like rotten twine. Bones splintered and cracked under the Geatish hero's relentless, ungodly pressure. 

Supreme glory belonged to Beowulf. 

Bleeding out and screaming in agony, Grendel tore himself free, forced to stumble down the muddy swamp slopes toward his miserable, joyless lair. The monster knew his time was up. His final hour had arrived.

The bloody battle was over. The Danes finally had their revenge. This savage warrior from a distant land had ripped Hrothgar's hall from the brink of total ruin. 

Beowulf basked in the bloody aftermath of his brutal night's work. The Geatish warlord had backed up his boast with savage action. In a single night, he had wiped away years of terror, distress, and sorrow for the Danes.

As the ultimate trophy, Beowulf hoisted his gruesome prize high under the soaring roof of Heorot. Grendel's severed hand, arm, and shredded shoulder hung there—the monster's massive claw on full display, dripping with the proof of a hero's absolute victory!
