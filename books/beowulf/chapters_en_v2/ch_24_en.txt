Chapter Twenty-Four: The Death of Aeschere and the Swamp of Monsters

> *The death of a favored comrade, the grief-stricken old king urgently summons the Geat hero.*

Hrothgar’s voice was grim. "Don't ask me of joy. Sorrow has clawed its way back into our lands. Aeschere is dead." 

The king’s eyes burned with grief. "He was my most trusted counselor. My brother-in-arms. We bled together on the battlefield, standing shoulder-to-shoulder while helmets shattered and swords broke around us. Aeschere was everything a warrior should be!"

Hrothgar clenched his fists. "A restless, murderous horror slaughtered him right here in Heorot. She dragged his corpse away, bloated with pride, off to feast in the dark. She came to settle the score for yesterday, when you crushed Grendel’s life out with your bare hands."

He paced the hall. "For years, Grendel butchered my people and bled my kingdom dry. You ended him. But now, another mighty abomination rises to avenge her son. Her vengeance cuts deep. The loyal hand that showered us with gifts and struck down our enemies now lies cold."

The king turned back to Beowulf. "My scouts speak of two giant stalkers haunting the moors. One twisted into the shape of a woman. The other, an outcast in the form of a man—only far more monstrous. The people called him Grendel. Who sired him, or what hellish spirits spawned before him, no one knows."

Hrothgar's tone grew darker. "They fester in a desolate wasteland of wolf-haunted ridges and razor-sharp cliffs. Out there, a mountain stream plunges beneath the jagged rock, vanishing deep underground. The lake lies just a few miles away. Frozen, gnarl-rooted trees loom over the water. And every night, the surface erupts in unnatural fire. No man alive knows its true depth."

He stepped closer, his voice dropping to a whisper. "Even a hunted stag, desperate and chased by hounds, will let the dogs tear its throat out on the bank rather than dive into that cursed water. It is a place of absolute dread. When storms strike, black waves surge to the clouds, choking the sky until the heavens weep."

Hrothgar locked eyes with the Geatish champion. "Once again, everything rests on you. You don’t know this killing ground, nor the nightmare lair where this creature hides. Seek it if you dare! Break her, and I will drown you in ancient treasure and twisted gold, just as I did before."
