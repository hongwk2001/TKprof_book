Chapter Thirty-One: The Farewell Feast

> *The feast of parting, the hero pledges eternal loyalty to the old king along with immense treasures.*

"For half a century, I crushed our enemies!" Hrothgar roared, his voice shaking the timber of the hall. "I shielded the Danes with cold iron and splintering spears. No warlord under the sky dared stand against me. But then the shadow fell."

The old king’s eyes darkened, remembering the slaughter. "Grendel shattered our joy. He tore apart my men and left a gnawing grief that bled my spirit dry. But today, the nightmare ends."

Hrothgar leveled a scarred finger at the monstrous, blood-crusted head resting on the stone floor. "Praise the Gods I lived long enough to stare into that bastard’s dead eyes! Take your seat, champion! Drink your fill. Tomorrow, we rip open the treasury and shower you in gold!"

Beowulf grinned, his knuckles still stained with monster blood, and strode to his bench. The mead flowed. The hall erupted into a roaring celebration as Geats and Danes feasted like conquering gods.

When pitch-black night swallowed the sky, the roaring fires died down to embers. The gray-haired king finally retreated to his chambers. Beowulf, battered and bone-weary from butchering sea-demons in the abyss, craved the quiet dark. A hall-warden hurried over, bowing low, and escorted the champion to a luxurious bedchamber.

The great-hearted hero slept like the dead.

Outside, the golden roof of Heorot pierced the night sky. Beowulf didn't stir until a black raven shrieked at the breaking dawn, heralding the sun.

Daylight burned away the shadows. The Geatish warriors leaped from their beds, their blood pumping. They were ready to hit the sea. It was time to go home.

Beowulf snatched up Hrunting and tossed the ancient blade back to Unferth.

"Thanks for the steel," Beowulf said, a wolfish grin on his face. "She’s a brutal companion in a bloodbath. Strong, reliable, and sharp as hell."

Even though the sword had failed him against the sea-hag, Beowulf played the flawless hero to the end.

Armed, armored, and bristling with weapons, the Geats marched back into the great hall. Beowulf stepped up to the high throne, ready to bid the wise king a final, legendary farewell.
