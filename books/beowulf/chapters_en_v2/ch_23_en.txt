Chapter Twenty-Three: The Revenge of Grendel's Mother

> *A blood-soaked revenge, the Swamp Hag who lost her son opens another night of slaughter.*

The Danish warriors crashed in Heorot. They thought the nightmare was over. 

They were dead wrong. One of them was about to pay the ultimate price for a good night's sleep. 

For years, Grendel had slaughtered at will, bringing nothing but blood and misery to the hall until Beowulf shattered him. But out in the black waters of the moors, an avenger was already plotting. Grendel was dead, but his mother wasn't. And she was boiling with grief and rage.

This monstrous hag festered in the frozen streams and black lakes, carrying the ancient curse of Cain. When the first murderer fled mankind's joy into the wasteland, he spawned a vile brood of demons—Grendel among them. 

But Grendel had crossed Beowulf. The demon thought he was hunting prey, but Beowulf was waiting in the dark. Armed with bone-crushing strength and absolute faith, Beowulf tore the beast apart and sent him crawling back to the swamp to die.

Now, the hell-dam wanted blood. 

She exploded into Heorot while the Danes slept completely unaware of the approaching slaughter. Her ambush sparked pure chaos. She might not have possessed the sheer, world-breaking strength of her son, but make no mistake—she was a lethal predator.

Warriors jolted awake in a blind panic. Swords ripped from scabbards, and men snatched their heavy shields. Nobody had a second to strap on armor or secure a helmet. The terror struck too fast. 

The moment she realized the hall was waking up, the monster decided to bail. But she wasn't leaving empty-handed. She lunged, burying her claws into one of the nobles, hoisted him up like a ragdoll, and bolted for the moors.

Her victim wasn't just some random guard. He was King Hrothgar's most trusted advisor and dearest friend—slaughtered in his sleep. 

Where was Beowulf? The mighty Geat wasn't there. After the feast, they had set him up in private quarters across the compound. 

Cries of horror echoed through Heorot as the surviving Danes realized the full scope of the devastation. Grendel's mother had not only murdered their brother-in-arms, but she had also ripped her son's severed, bloody arm down from the rafters. 

The nightmare was back. A bloody exchange of lives.

King Hrothgar was utterly shattered when he got the news. The old king demanded Beowulf be summoned immediately. 

At the crack of dawn, the champion marched into the royal chambers, his heavy boots shaking the wooden floorboards. Oblivious to the nighttime slaughter, Beowulf offered a cheerful greeting.

"My Lord," Beowulf hailed. "Did you rest well?" 

Little did he know, rest was the last thing the King of the Danes had found that night.
