Chapter Eighteen: The Spoils of Carnage

> *The festival of victory, the songs of great heroes heat up the golden hall.*

Beowulf, son of Ecgtheow, stood tall and grinned. "We threw down with the beast and loved every second of it! We stared that nightmare right in the eyes. I just wish you could have seen Grendel yourself—choking on his own blood, broken and dying!"

He raised a scarred fist. "I meant to slam him into a deathbed right here on the floor. I locked my grip to crush the life out of him, but the bastard slipped my hold. The Almighty didn't want him to die in this hall, and the fiend bolted before I could snap his spine."

Beowulf gestured to the bloody trophy hanging from the rafters. "But he left his hand, arm, and shoulder to buy his pathetic retreat. Not that it’ll save him. That miserable freak is already a walking corpse. Agony has him by the throat, chained in a death grip he can’t break. Now he’s crawling in the dark, waiting for God to throw him into the abyss!"

Unferth, son of Ecglaf, suddenly had nothing to say. The loudmouth's boasts dried up completely. 

The nobles crowded in, staring up at the high roof. They gaped at the severed arm Beowulf had ripped from the monster. Grendel's fingers ended in brutal, steel-hard talons. Those monstrous spikes were designed to shred flesh and bone. Every warrior in the room knew the truth: no sword on earth, no matter how ancient or sharp, could have hacked through that unholy hide. Only raw, god-given strength had done the job.

With the beast defeated, orders flew out to restore Heorot. Men and women swarmed the great hall, scrubbing away the gore. They hung brilliant, gold-woven tapestries across the bloodstained walls, turning a slaughterhouse back into a palace. 

The sheer violence of the brawl had nearly wrecked the building. Iron-forged beams were twisted, and the massive door hinges were completely shredded. Only the roof had survived intact when the demonic outlaw violently broke out and ran for his life. 

But death is a hunter you cannot outrun! Every soul on earth must face the grave when the feast of life finally ends.

Soon, Hrothgar strode into the great hall. The King of the Danes had come to celebrate. Never had a more savage and loyal band of warriors gathered around their warlord! The legendary heroes claimed their benches and tore into the feast. 

Hrothgar and his fierce nephew, Hrothulf, slammed their cups together, draining rivers of mead in the high hall. Heorot roared with the laughter of brothers-in-arms. For now, the Danes stood united, their blades sharp and their hearts completely free of betrayal.
