Chapter Seventeen: The King's Praise

> *Footprints leading to the bloody swamp, confirming the miserable end of the beast that fled to hell.*

Sigemund claimed the title of the world's most famous adventurer. His violent, glorious deeds cemented his early legend. But ancient King Heremod? His power rotted. His courage crumbled. Betrayed to his enemies in the land of the Jutes, Heremod met a swift and brutal end. 

Crippled by his own sorrow, Heremod had devolved into a deadly curse upon his own people. His nobles watched him fall. The wise men mourned the king he used to be, praying he would finally rise and defend their gold and their homeland. He never did. 

Sin and greed devoured Heremod’s heart. But Beowulf, the fierce nephew of Hygelac, forged a different path, earning the roaring love of his friends and the awe of mankind.

Outside, warriors raced their warhorses down the dirt roads, kicking up dust in the blinding morning sun. A massive crowd of battle-hardened men surged toward the great hall. They craved a glimpse of the prize—the severed, bloody claw of the monster.

King Hrothgar, the scarred guardian of the kingdom’s hoard, marched from the royal chambers flanked by a heavy troop of loyal nobles. Queen Wealhtheow strode fiercely at his side, a company of maidens at her back, heading straight for the feast-hall.

Hrothgar ascended the hall's steps. He planted his boots firmly, staring up at the gleaming golden roof, then down at the grotesque ruin of Grendel’s arm. 

His voice boomed out over the crowd.

"Thank the Almighty for this bloody prize! Grendel dragged us through hell. He drowned us in suffering. But the Guardian of Glory still forges miracles!" Hrothgar roared. "I nearly gave up. I watched this beautiful hall get painted with our own blood. Even our wisest counselors broke down, convinced we could never defend our home from these nightmare demons!"

Hrothgar turned his blazing eyes to the crowd. "But now, by the power of the Lord, one young warrior has smashed through where all our clever plans failed! If the mother who bore this titan still breathes, she knows God smiled upon her!"

He pointed straight at the hero. "Beowulf! Best of men! From this day forward, I claim you as my own son. Guard this bond with your life. Ask for anything in my power, and it is yours!"

Hrothgar threw his arms wide. "I have dumped chests of treasure on lesser men who couldn't fight half as well as you. But you? You carved your own glory into eternity! May the Almighty reward your fury, today and forever!"
