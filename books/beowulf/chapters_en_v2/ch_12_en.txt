King Hrothgar took his leave. With his elite guard in tow, the old king abandoned the great hall of Heorot to join Queen Wealhtheow in her bedchamber.

> *Doom arrives tearing through the pitch-black darkness, the hero silently waits for the monster's throat.*

He left the defense of the hall to a different kind of guard. Beowulf stepped up to hold the line in the dark.

The Geat warlord didn't rely on steel. He trusted his own savage strength. Stripping off his heavy iron mail-shirt, Beowulf tossed his helmet aside. He unbelted his legendary, rune-carved sword and shoved it into his servant's hands.

"Guard my gear," Beowulf ordered. 

Before he hit his cot, the hero turned to his men and laid down his final boast. 

"I don't bleed any easier than Grendel!" he growled. "I won't use a blade to gut this beast, even though it would be easy work. The monster doesn't know how to parry or block. He only knows slaughter. So tonight, no weapons. If he wants a fistfight, I'll give him a fistfight. Let God Almighty pick the winner!"

Beowulf dropped onto his bed, his head hitting the pillow. All around him, his hardened sea-raiders crashed on the mead-hall floor. Sleep took them, but it was a grim rest. Not a single man believed he would ever see his homeland again. The floorboards beneath them were still stained with the blood of slaughtered Danes. 

But destiny had other plans. The gods of war wove a bloody victory for the Geats, deciding that one man's raw, unstoppable might would crush the nightmare of Heorot. 

Deep in the black night, the shadow moved. Grendel came gliding down from the misty moors, hunting for flesh. 

Inside the hall, the men tasked with defending it were dead to the world in sleep. All except one. 

Beowulf lay wide awake in the suffocating dark. His blood boiled. His heart hammered like a war drum. He stared into the shadows, fists clenched, waiting for the slaughter to begin.
