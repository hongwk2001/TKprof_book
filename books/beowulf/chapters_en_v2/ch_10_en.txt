Chapter Ten: Beowulf's Swimming Contest with Breca

> *The hospitality of Queen Wealhtheow, the hero swears victory or a glorious death.*

Beowulf, son of Ecgtheow, locked eyes with Unferth and laughed. "Listen to yourself, Unferth. The beer is doing the talking for you. You run your mouth about Breca and our swimming race, but let's get the facts straight: nobody on this earth has fought harder or survived worse in the churning sea than me.

"When we were boys, we made a reckless bet to risk our lives on the open ocean. We didn't back down. We hit the icy water with drawn swords in our hands, ready to butcher any sea-beasts that wanted a taste. Breca couldn't outswim me, and I refused to leave him behind. We battled the currents shoulder-to-shoulder for five brutal nights.

"Then the storm hit. 

"Freezing darkness fell like a hammer. A savage north wind ripped the ocean apart, churning the waves into a nightmare. 

"The blood-scent drove the sea monsters insane. But my forged chain-mail held strong. A leviathan grabbed me in its crushing grip and dragged me down into the abyss. It thought I was prey. It was dead wrong. I drove my blade through its black heart and gutted the beast on the ocean floor.

"More monsters swarmed me in the black depths. I hacked them to pieces. They thought they'd feast on my flesh at the bottom of the sea. Instead, when the sun rose, their mangled corpses washed up on the shoreline, butchered by my sword. They'll never hunt sailors in the deep again.

"As the dawn broke and God's light burned across the sky, the raging waves died down. The cliffs appeared. Fate spares a warrior whose time hasn't come—if he's got the guts to fight! By the time it was over, I had slaughtered nine sea-fiends. I have never fought a bloodier battle under the stars, nor has any man survived worse odds in the abyss. I dragged myself onto the beaches of the Finns, utterly drained but alive.

"Now, Unferth, I've never heard anyone sing about your bloody sword fights! Neither you nor Breca have ever done anything remotely as daring. I don't need to boast, but I will speak the truth: your only claim to fame is murdering your own brothers! You'll burn in hell for that, no matter how clever you think you are.

"Face the facts, Unferth. Grendel would never have butchered your people and humiliated your king if your blade was half as sharp as your tongue. He knows he doesn't have to fear the Danes. He slaughters you, feasts on you, and walks right out, laughing at your weakness. 

"But playtime is over. I'm going to show this monster the raw, skull-crushing power of the Geats! And when the sun rises tomorrow, anyone who wants to drink in this hall will do it in peace."
