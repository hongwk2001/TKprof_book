# Translation Guide: The Blue Castle (푸른 성)

**1. The Blue Castle (푸른 성) — L.M. Montgomery (1926)**

**The "Web Novel" Trope:** Rebellion, Terminal Illness (Fake-out), Contract Romance, and Cozy Escapism.

**Why it is a Commercial Goldmine:**
In Korea, the "Romance Fantasy" (로판) web novel genre is a massive multi-million dollar industry. *The Blue Castle* reads exactly like a modern Korean web novel. The protagonist is a 29-year-old woman treated as an "old maid" by her toxic, overbearing family. Upon believing she has a terminal illness, she decides to rebel, speak her mind, move into a cozy cabin in the Canadian wilderness, and marry a mysterious social outcast.

**Digital Marketing Strategy:**
Market this under the Ridi Books romance category. Use keywords like:
- "Cozy Romance" (힐링 로맨스)
- "Rebellious Protagonist" (사이다 여주 - meaning "refreshing like Sprite")
- "Forest House Living"

Since Montgomery is famous for *Anne of Green Gables*, the author's name carries immediate search engine optimization (SEO) value, but the plot of *The Blue Castle* is vastly more adult and romantic.

## Formatting & TTS Compatibility Rules
- **No Parentheses for Explanations**: Do not use parentheses to explain foreign concepts or English words (e.g., "퐁파두르(앞머리를 높게 띄운 스타일)"). Text-to-Speech (TTS) engines read out punctuation ("괄호 열고... 괄호 닫고"), which ruins the audiobook immersion.
- **Natural Integration**: Weave explanations directly into the sentence naturally. (e.g., "앞머리를 빵빵하게 부풀리는 퐁파두르 스타일").
- **Pronunciation over Spelling**: Use transliterations for English words if the literal Korean translation sounds awkward for TTS (e.g., use "블루 캐슬" instead of "푸른 성(Blue Castle)").
