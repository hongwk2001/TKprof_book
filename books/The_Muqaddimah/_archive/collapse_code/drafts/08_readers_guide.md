# Appendix A: Reader's Guide to the Original Muqaddimah

Because this book is a modern retelling aimed at presenting Ibn Khaldun’s most vital concepts to a commercial audience, we have condensed and restructured his theories into 7 fast-paced chapters. 

However, we want to maintain absolute scholarly integrity. If you are a student of history, economics, or political science and wish to dive into the massive, 1,500-page original text of *The Muqaddimah*, you should know exactly where our modern concepts came from. 

The original *Muqaddimah* is divided into 6 "Books" (or major chapters). Here is exactly how our modern retelling maps onto Ibn Khaldun's 14th-century academic structure:

### The Original 6-Part Structure

**Book One: Human Civilization in General**
*Ibn Khaldun spends a massive amount of time covering geography, climate, and meteorology here. We bypassed the climate science to extract his foundational definitions of society.*

**Book Two: Bedouin Civilization (Savage Nations and Tribes)**
* **Our Book:** This is the source for **Chapter 2: What Is Asabiyyah?** and **Chapter 3: How Empires Are Born**. Here is where Ibn Khaldun establishes the psychological toughness of the desert and the raw power of Asabiyyah.

**Book Three: On Dynasties, Royal Authority, and The Caliphate**
* **Our Book:** This is the absolute core of Ibn Khaldun's political and economic theory. This massive section is the source text for:
    * **Chapter 4: The Luxury Trap**
    * **Chapter 5: Elite Overproduction** (The taxation death spiral and the hiring of mercenaries).
    * **Chapter 6: The 3-Generation Rule**

**Book Four: Countries and Cities**
*Ibn Khaldun explores urban planning, the architecture of cities, and how the physical layout of a sedentary civilization affects its population. We drew heavily on his contrast between "the desert" and "the city" from this section.*

**Book Five: On Making a Living (Economics and Arts)**
*This section covers the mechanics of trade, profit, agriculture, and craftsmanship. His theories on labor and value heavily influenced our understanding of his economic worldview.*

**Book Six: Sciences and the Kinds of Instruction**
*A massive encyclopedia of all known human knowledge in the 14th century, covering everything from theology and poetry to mathematics and the occult.* 

If you wish to read the original text, we highly recommend the 1958 English translation by Franz Rosenthal, which remains the academic gold standard. But beware: it is not for the faint of heart!
