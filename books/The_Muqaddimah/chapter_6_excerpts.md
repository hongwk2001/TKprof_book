# Chapter 6: The 3-Generation Rule (Source Excerpts & Translation)

Here are the exact passages where Ibn Khaldun lays out his famous rule that empires collapse after three generations, translated into the punchy, modern English style designed for our book.

*(Note: In the original text, these passages repeat in two different volumes, which is why they appear 6 times. The core argument is perfectly preserved below).*

---

### Excerpt 1: The Lifespan of an Empire
**Original Arabic (Line 12742):**
> وأما أعمار الدول أيضاء وإن كان يختلف بحسب القرانات» إلا أن الدولة في الغالب لا تعدو أعمار ثلاثة أجيال. والجيل هو عمر شخص واحد من العمر الوسطء فيكون أربعين الذي هو انتهاء النمو والنشوء إلى غايته.

**Modern Book Translation:**
> "While the exact lifespan of a dynasty can vary depending on political circumstances, as a general rule, **no empire lasts beyond three generations**. A 'generation' is the average lifespan of a single person—roughly forty years, the time it takes for a human being to reach their absolute physical and mental peak."

*(Note for book: This gives the empire a total lifespan of roughly 120 years before the inevitable collapse).*

---

### Excerpt 2: The First Generation (The Hungry Founders)
**Original Arabic (Line 12761 / 55628):**
> وإنما قلنا أن عمر الدولة في الغالب لا يعدو ثلاثة أجيال» لأن الجيل الأول لم يزالوا على شُلق البداوة وخشونتها وتوحّشها من شظف العيش» والبسالة» والافتراس» والاشتراك في المجد. فلا تزال بذلك سَّوْرة العصبية محفوظة فيهم. فحدّهم مُرهِف»ء وجانبهم مَرُهوب.ء والناس لهم مغلوبون.

**Modern Book Translation:**
> "Why exactly three generations? Because the **First Generation** retains the tough, savage qualities of their desert origins. They are forged by the harshness of life. They are brave, predatory, and they share the glory of their conquests equally. As long as these conditions exist, their *Asabiyyah* (social cohesion) remains incredibly powerful. Their swords are sharp, they are feared by their enemies, and other nations submit to them."

---

### Excerpt 3: The Second Generation (The Luxury Trap)
**Original Arabic (Line 12761 continued):**
> والجيل الثاني تحوّل حالهم بالملك والرفه من البداوة إلى الحضارة...

**Modern Book Translation:**
> "But by the **Second Generation**, everything changes. Thanks to the absolute power and massive wealth their fathers won, their lifestyle shifts from the tough nomadic desert to the luxury of the city. Power is no longer shared; it is hoarded by one central ruler. The fierce solidarity of the founders begins to crack, but the empire survives because the second generation still remembers the struggles of the first."

---

### Excerpt 4: The Third Generation (The Collapse)
**Original Arabic (Line 12805 / 55653):**
> فهذه» كما تراه» ثلاثة أجيال فيها يكون هرم الدولة وتخلقها. ولذلك كان انقراض الحسب في الجيل الرابع ... وهذه الأجيال الثلاثة أعمارها مائة وعشرون سنة» على ما مر. ولا تعدو ويصطنع من يغني عن الدولة بعض الغناء» حتى يأذن الله بانقراضها فتذهب الدولة ما حملت.

**Modern Book Translation:**
> "By the **Third Generation**, the nomadic toughness is entirely forgotten. They are completely consumed by luxury and soft living. They can no longer defend themselves, forcing the ruler to hire outsiders and mercenaries to protect the state. As you can see, it takes exactly three generations for an empire to grow old, decay, and die. At this point, the empire is merely waiting for God to permit its destruction, and it will be swept away by a new, hungrier group."

### How to Use This in the Chapter
This is incredibly powerful material for the book. We can structure Chapter 6 by dropping these exact translations as pull-quotes, and then expanding on them with:
1. **The Roman Empire** (the tough republicans vs. the decadent late emperors).
2. **Modern Business** (The famous saying: "Shirtsleeves to shirtsleeves in three generations" where the founder builds the wealth, the son maintains it, and the grandson blows it).
3. **Modern Nations** (Applying this 120-year cycle to modern superpower dynamics).
